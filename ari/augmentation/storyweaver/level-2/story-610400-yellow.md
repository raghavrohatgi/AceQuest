# Yellow

"Lin Lin!" "Yes Bei?"

"Linlin,&nbsp; Bei is leaving.&nbsp; You be a good girl and listen to your Beipun, okay?" "Okay, Bei. Bye bye!"

"Why are Bei’s hands so yellow but not mine?"

"Let’s see where Bei is headed and find out why!"

"There she is!"

"Whoa!&nbsp; &nbsp;What is this?"

"Hey there, little girl! Did I just hear a question?" "Who are you?"

"I am the one and only Ma Lakadong – the only turmeric with a curcumin content of 6–12% in the whole world! Have you heard of me?" "No!"

"No? Wha-wha-what about the story of the magic spice and cows then?" &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;"Magic…?"

"Yes! Let me tell you." "A long time ago, during your ancestors' time, a herd of cows were attacked by tigers!" "Your ancestors, looking for medical herbs, found me in the forest with my peculiar smell…"

"…they took me and applied my magic yellow paste on the injured cows." "And of course, I healed them! That’s how I was discovered! Didn’t I tell ya? I’m magic!"

"Whoaa. Magic! Ma Lakadong, are you also the reason why my Bei’s hands are yellow?" "Oh yes, I do leave a beautiful shade of yellow on the people I fancy."

"I want my hands to be yellow like Bei!" "Leave it to me!"

"Linlin! What are you doing here?" "Bei! I was just talking to &nbsp; Ma Lakadong…Huh?" Ma Lakadong was&nbsp; nowhere to be seen.

…but when she looked down to her hands, she was surprised to see that her hands were a beautiful yellow…

…just like her Bei’s!

Take half a spoon of turmeric in a bowl Add two spoons of water and stir Apply the paste on your palm. Make sure your hands are painted yellow like Linlin and her mother! Lay your hand on a color paper of your choice. Press down for a minute. Activity Let's meet Ma Lakadong!

Wait for it to dry Draw eyes for Ma Lakadong Cut along the shape And you have &nbsp;your very own&nbsp; Ma Lakadong!

● Lakadong Turmeric is renowned for its exceptionally high curcumin content, ranging from 6-12%, making it one of the varieties with the highest curcumin levels. It has a darker shade and is grown organically. ● On December 2023, Lakadong Turmeric received a Geographical Indication (GI) tag, signifying its unique qualities and origin in the West Jaintia Hills district of Meghalaya, India. ● Around 14,000 farmers, in West Jaintia Hills, are currently engaged in cultivating Lakadong turmeric on 1,753 hectares of land. &nbsp; ● It was traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects, and still has high medicinal values from treating cancer to enhancing cognitive health. ● It is used in local cuisines for its distinctive flavor and aroma. ● It is used extensively in cosmetics as well. Facts about Lakadong Turmeric ﻿* *Information extracted from Mission Lakadong 2018–2023, Directorate of Horticulture, Department of Agriculture, Government of Meghalaya.

GLOSSARY Bei : &nbsp;a Pnar word for Mother Beipun : a Pnar word for Grandmother Ma : A Pnar word for uncle or any older man in the community

# A Garo Kitchen

CHU'DAP A traditional fireplace where food is cooked.

The Chu'dap varies from household to household.

DIKKA An earthenware vessel designed specifically to store fermented rice beer or wine. ONGGARE A rack constructed above the fireplace to store items.

GAN'SRANG&nbsp; Used for storing utensils and various household items. TE'BIL BIJAK Used to encase rice and curry and placed on a gan'srang. This is eaten for lunch.

GAN'SRANG A storage rack for utensils and other kitchen items.

KOKSI This vessel is used while fishing to store the caught fish.

&nbsp;KERANG A basket made of bamboo designed for transporting rice.

&nbsp;JANGGA A storage area specifically designed for the preservation and storage of smoked meats. This also allows them to extend their shelf life.

SANGGI&nbsp; An earthen pot used for storing water.

BASING An earthen pot used for storing water.

MERONG RONGDIK Earthen vessel or pot used for storing rice.

MERAKU/TOMBOL Corn stored for its seeds which are then used for sowing. MISI Millet stored and preserved for its seeds

MATGOL Used for smoking and drying meat.

NA'KAM WA'SING&nbsp; A hollow vessel made of bamboo designed to store fermented dry fish.

‘A Garo Kitchen,’ is one of the three books which is part of 'The Kitchen Series.' 'The Kitchen Series,' is&nbsp; part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative by the Sauramandala Foundation. The TFFP team is grateful to the families and individuals of Babadam village for opening their homes and sharing about the different types of kitchen items used by the people of the Garo community. Without them, this book would not have been possible.

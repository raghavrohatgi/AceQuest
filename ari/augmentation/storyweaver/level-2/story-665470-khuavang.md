# Khuavang

It was like any other day in 3C. Dari and Kimi were enjoying their book amidst the chatter in the classroom.

Suddenly, everything stopped.

The tap, tap, tap of the pencils. The screech, screech, screech of the chairs. The rustle, rustle, rustle of shoes. All the sounds seemed to have suddenly disappeared.

Even the sunlight felt different, as though the sun itself had paused. An eerie silence spread across the room, and Dari felt her heart quicken. “What is going on?” she thought to herself.

She turned to Kimi. Kimi’s eyes were wide. Soundlessly, her mouth moved, and she whispered out the words. “It’s a khuavang kal lai.”

Dari’s eyes widened. She had heard of the khuavangs once — strange creatures her grandmother had once told her about. “When there is a moment of complete silence, the khuavangs are believed to walk among us, unseen but present.” “What is a khuavang?” Dari had asked. “They are creatures of nature, you see. You can’t see them, but you can feel their presence. When there is pin-drop silence, almost like magic, that is when you know it is khuavang kal lai ,” grandmother had said.

Was she going to get a glimpse of the khuavang today? In the suffocating stillness, Dari’s eyes began to wander.

Maybe they were really, really big, taking up all the space in the classroom. Or maybe they are very, very small, which is why they cannot be seen. “Could they be sitting on Kimi’s head?”

Maybe they liked to fly in the air. Maybe that was why it was so dim. Or maybe, they prefer the ground, silently crawling through all the stillness.

With every passing second, Dari’s heart quickened. Is that a flicker? Was that a spot up there? Should she make a sound? What if they heard her?

Cough, cough, cough! Gasp! Someone coughed at the back of the class, and then the class was alive again. Tap, tap, tap. Screech, screech, screech. Rustle, rustle, rustle. Dari let out a deep breath. “That was scary, wasn’t it?” Kimi said.

Dari nodded as she looked at the boy who had just coughed. A shiver went down her spine. Right there on his forehead was a small, round, dark mole. It had not been there earlier.

“Looks like he’s been marked. That’s the khuavang chhinchhiah ,” Kimi said in a low voice. “Marked?” Dari asked. “That’s right,” Kimi said. “He was the first to break the silence, so they left him with a little mark. It’s not a bad thing, really, just a little reminder from the khuavangs to honour their stillness.”

“Hmmm, a reminder.” Dari whispered. Was the khuavang real, or had she just imagined the whole thing? Either way, she now knew that the silence of the khuavangs should be honoured.

Just as she sat, ready to get back to her book, something caught her eye. Right on top of her left hand sat a clear round black mole. She too had received the khuavang’s mark.

About the Story In Mizo folklore, the Khuavangs are mythical creatures believed to be unseen but ever-present, especially during moments of unnatural stillness. They are often associated with controlling nature or possessing magical powers. They are typically seen as benign and even benevolent, blessing humans with fertility, a long life, and prosperity. Khuavang Kal Lai is a term from Mizo folklore meaning ‘pin-drop silence’, it refers to a moment when the Khuavangs are near, and the world around falls completely silent. It is said to happen when the Khuavangs walk among humans, and the silence is an indication of their presence. Khuavang Chhinchhiah or the ‘mark of the Khuavang’ manifests as a small, dark mole or mark on a person’s body. It is believed to appear when someone has been touched by the Khuavang, particularly after they have broken the silence caused by their presence.

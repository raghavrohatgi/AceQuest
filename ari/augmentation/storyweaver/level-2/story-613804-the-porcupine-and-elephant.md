# The Porcupine and Elephant

My oh my!&nbsp; What a lovely day for a bath.

Who dares anger a mighty creature like me, the Elephant!! If you dare, show yourself!

Who are you to question what I do?&nbsp; Fear me, as I am mightier than you!

Hah! Mightier than me? Not a cha nce!

I am sending a strand of my hair.&nbsp; See for yourself if you would like to face me.

Hahahahahaha! Whoooaaa! Thud!

And that is why rats have very prominent front teeth.&nbsp;

Activity &nbsp; &nbsp; Can you spot the rat in the following images?

About the Story &nbsp; An amazing thing about oral traditions is the fact that there are different variations of the same story. Stories narrated orally are passed down from one generation to the next and variations appear because people/regions adapt a story, add or subtract plots, characters, etc. based on their need and understanding. This particular story was pieced together based on two people’s narrations–same story with variations in the endings!

'The Porcupine and Elephant,’ is a part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative of the Sauramandala Foundation.&nbsp; The TFFP team is grateful to Rufus Pangrak G Momin and Bikramjit A Sangma for sharing their versions of the folktale without which this book would not have been possible.

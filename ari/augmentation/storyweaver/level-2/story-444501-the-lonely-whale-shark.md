# The Lonely Whale Shark

I am a big little whale shark&nbsp; &nbsp;and I am sad on my reef.

For this turtle has a friend. This jellyfish does too.

Even these remoras swim together into the blue.

Why am I the only lonely fish?

Oh! Look! It is the coming together of whale sharks from everywhere, with very exciting stories to share.

I saw an octopus eat a crab. I spoke to a really old sea turtle. A shark with sharp teeth tried to attack me! Have you seen a sunfish? I saw two on my way over here.

If I look hard, maybe I will see it too. For there is plenty of fish I have bumped into. Whether on the reef or in the open ocean, if I pay attention, there is enough commotion.

I have met a grouper with a funny bone, and a fish that I confused for a stone. A squid that was terribly shy, and a manta ray that thought it could fly!

I am a big little whale shark...

and I am not so sad anymore. At least, not as sad as I was before.

For even though I travel on my own, I am never truly alone.

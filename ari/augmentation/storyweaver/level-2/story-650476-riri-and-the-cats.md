# Riri and the Cats

In a cottage near the town, Riri lived with her parents and their ginger cat Meomeo. Meomeo had two lovely little kittens. Riri adored Meomeo and her kittens.

The kittens were very playful and Riri loved to watch them. They would tumble around the kitchen and catch the toys she threw at them.

In the courtyard they stalked and chased the chicks until the mother hen pecked at them.

Whenever Riri played with the kittens, Meomeo would sit and watch them have their fun. Sometimes, she would purr against Riri, encouraging her to help her train her kittens. However, some nights the kittens would stop all movement and just stare at the sky with their mother.

Like their mother, both the kittens had curious emerald eyes. Riri’s mother would often say, “curiosity is a sign of wisdom.”

One night Riri found Meomeo and her kittens gazing at the sky once again.

“Av a , Meomeo and her family are watching TV in the sky again. What are they looking at?” “Watching TV?” Av a &nbsp;laughed. “Ohhh, she is&nbsp; teaching her kittens how to count the stars.” “Count stars? But there are&nbsp; so&nbsp; many&nbsp; of&nbsp; them,” Riri said.

“Well, cats have their own wisdom. On starry night like tonight, you will usually find them sitting on high places like a firewood pile, tree stumps, or rocks, and gazing at the stars, counting them. Cats like high places, you know, because they are phila-wungnao,” Ava explained. “What is phila-wungnao?&nbsp; ” asked Riri.

“It means having royal status like being a queen or princess. It is said that among the animals, the cat belongs to the royal family. That is why cats cannot be bought or sold. Meomeo was gifted to us by your aunty, remember?” “Oh yes!” Riri exclaimed.

“Meomeo is so smart. Is she smarter than all of us, Ava?” Riri asked. “Well, she is smart but it does not mean she is smarter than all of us,” Ava replied. “Really?” “Yes, while cats can the count stars, they cannot count the hearthstones,” Ava explained. What? But it’s only three. Why can’t they count three stones?” Riri remarked.

“When Meomeo wants to count the hearthstones,&nbsp; she jumps and sits on one of the hearthstones and counts the ones she can see. One...two… Then, she would leap onto the next hearthstone and again count…one…two.”

“She leaps from one hearthstone to another, but never counts the hearthstone she just sat on&nbsp; because she can’t see it. And because of this, for Meomeo, there are only two hearthstones,” Ava said.

“That is so funny,” Riri laughed, “it is so simple but she cannot count it!”&nbsp; Tiptoeing towards the cats, Riri cupped her hands around her mouth and declared loudly. “There are three hearthstones. There are only three. Do not forget that!” “MEOW!!!!”&nbsp; The cats turned to Riri, scolding her for the rude interruption.

“Don’t worry Meomeo, I will teach you how to count the hearthstones.&nbsp; In turn, you also show me how to count stars,” Riri said, gently stroking their backs.&nbsp; “Meow,” Meomeo purred.

From that day onwards, while the kittens played, Riri got busy teaching Meomeo how to count the hearthstones. “One…two…three.” “One…two…three.” “One…two…three.”

“Do not forget to count the stone you sit on.” “Meow”

But on starry nights Riri would join the cats staring at the sky,&nbsp; trying to count the stars. However, on such nights, Meomeo would just sit and purr, as if to say. “Try as you might, you will never know how to count the stars just like&nbsp; I will never understand how to count hearthstones.”

About the Story This story is based on three popular old sayings of the Tangkhul Naga tribe of Manipur about the cat. The main theme of the the story is crafted around the saying Lāmina sirā shānshapa kha meithalung machākkhuitheimana where it is said that a cat can count the stars but cannot count the hearthstones. Along with this saying, the story also touches upon two more sayings about the cat, lāmiva philā-wungnao kho (cats are of a royal lineage) and lāmi loshār yorshār kho , k a lāshāt&nbsp; ngaphanga (cats cannot be bought or sold, otherwise misfortune happens).

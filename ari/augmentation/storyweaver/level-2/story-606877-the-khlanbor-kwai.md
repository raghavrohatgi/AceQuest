# The Khlanbor Kwai

Sleep in the soil ripe little kwai

Shoot, roots, come! Don’t be shy

Up and up I grow into a tree

Leaves, trunk, and a kwai or three

A sim longlit sings&nbsp; a song&nbsp; The sun shares&nbsp; her&nbsp; light

Laroit , have built&nbsp; a nest&nbsp; Weng weng hugs&nbsp; me tight

A Khynnah Khynro daydreams&nbsp; The cool breeze blows

Some leaves fall Some friends go

Now I am thirty&nbsp; metres tall See, I can touch the sky!

It’s my friends who make me The Khlanbor Kwai!

Glossary Kwai:&nbsp; A betel nut. Khlanbor:&nbsp; &nbsp;A Pnar word for 'mighty.' This word is used since the trees are tall and live for more than fifty years. Sim longlit: A Pnar word for parrot. Laroit: A Pnar word for bulbul. Weng weng : A Pnar word for cicada. Khynnah Khynro: A teenage boy.

'The Khlanbor Kwai,' is part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative by the Sauramandala Foundation. The TFFP team is grateful to Mr. Bnai Syngkren of Shnongpdeng village for sharing extensive insights about the Betel Nut, without which this book would not have been possible.

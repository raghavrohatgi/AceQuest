# How Do I Feel

Everyday I walk to school with her.&nbsp; Today we have a History exam.&nbsp; I ask Ritu many questions. " "Pranam , my name is Ankita.&nbsp; I am in grade eight.&nbsp; My friend in school is Ritu.&nbsp; She always helps me.&nbsp; I feel happy and loved when I am with her.

" When we reach school, the bell rings.&nbsp; ‘Tunnn Tunnn Tunn’&nbsp; How will I remember so many dates?&nbsp; I’m worried but I close my books and run to class. "

" During the exam, I write two long answers but I forget the date of First War of Independence.&nbsp; I think and think but I can’t remember.&nbsp; Was it 1847? Was it 1857? Was it 1897? "

" Just then Rana Sir switches on the fan.&nbsp; Ritu’s answer sheet falls on the floor.&nbsp; I look at it by mistake.&nbsp; I see that she has underlined 1857. "

‘Two more minutes left’, says Rana Sir.&nbsp; ‘Tick tick. Tick tick.’ &nbsp; I am scared I will fail.&nbsp; I change the date and give my paper to Rana Sir. &nbsp;After exams, Ritu tries to call me but I run out of class.

" Oh no! What if Ritu finds out I cheated?&nbsp; What if I get more marks than Ritu? &nbsp;What if she does not&nbsp; want to be my friend?&nbsp; I am sad and scared. "

" I walk home alone.&nbsp; When I reach home, mummy asks me how my exam was.&nbsp; I start crying. " &nbsp; ‘Wahh Wahh Wahh’

" Mummy is confused so I tell her everything.&nbsp; Mummy listens carefully. She smiles at me. " ‘It is okay Anku, you looked at it by mistake.&nbsp; If you tell Ritu yourself she will understand.’ "

" In the evening I go to Ritu’s house. I tell her everything and say sorry. First, she makes a serious face. Then she smiles. ‘It is alright Anku, it was a mistake I understand. Do you want to tell Asma Madam?’ asks Ritu. ‘I am scared to tell her. What if she thinks I am a bad student?’ I ask. ‘Anku, Asma Madam will understand. You were scared to tell me but don’t you feel better after saying the truth?’ ‘Yes, I feel better I can be honest with Madam too. Tomorrow I will speak with her after class.’ "

‘Asma Madam, I have to tell you something.’ ‘Yes Anku, tell me,’ says Asma Madam. ‘In the History exam, I forgot one date. Rana Sir switched on the class fan and Ritu’s paper flew toward me. I saw the date on her paper and gave my answer. I feel bad about cheating. I am sorry Madam.’

‘Anku exams are to understand your learnings to provide you the right support,’ says Asma Madam. ‘I understand ma’am, this time I also learnt to ask for help and take responsibility for my actions.’ ‘Yes, today you learned from your mistakes,’ says Asma Madam. ‘Yes Madam, this will not happen again,’ I tell Ma’am. &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; ‘Thank you for sharing this with me, I trust you,’ says Asma Madam.

I walk back home with Ritu. ‘You were right Ritu. Asma Madam understands and forgives me.&nbsp; Thank you for encouraging me.’ I smile and hug Ritu. What makes you experience so many feelings?

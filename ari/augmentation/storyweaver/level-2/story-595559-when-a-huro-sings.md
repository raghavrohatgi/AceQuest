# When a Huro Sings

One morning in the Garo Hills!

Good morning Huro

Open your eyes Nono Sing with huro

Huro is singing Bum! Bum!

Simsang is dancing

Huro is pooping Yuck! Yuck!

Techri is blooming

Huro is clapping Clap! Clap!

Mongma is bathing

Pagipa cooks michu

Ambi sows rice

Ma · gipa got a client Oh wow! So nice!

Huro is happy today &nbsp; Up bright blue skies

If no more huro No singing Nono!

If no more huro Forest gone Nono!

When you care for huro

You care for Garo Hills

Open your &nbsp; eyes Nono &nbsp; &nbsp; Sing with huro

Open your eyes Nono Sing with huro

Hello! We are Huros! Can we tell you a little more about us? Are we monkeys? We are apes! Our other name is Western Hoolock Gibbons. Male huros are black in colour. Female huros are grey-brown. Where do we stay? Garo Hills is one of our homes. We live in the forest on top of the trees! Our favourite food? Flowers, leaves, fruits, and vegetables. We love caterpillars and spiders too. Yum! Our favourite thing to do? Singing and taking care of the Garo Hills. How do we take care of Garo Hills? Our poop helps trees grow and flowers bloom. Our songs make the forests feel good.

What to do when you see a huro? Tell us your name! Sing a song! But please stand far away, we are shy. How do huros get hurt? Some bad people hurt us with guns. Some lock us in their homes. Some cut down our homes. Electric shock from electric wires also hurts us. What if you see a huro who is hurt? Call an adult you trust! Like your teacher or your parents. Ask them to call our friends at The Huro Programme. &nbsp; Come, make a huro promise! I promise that I will always be a friend of the huros. I will tell all my friends, that huros are our friends too. And just like the huros, I will take care of our Garo Hills.

Glossary Garo Hills: &nbsp; A range of hills in Meghalaya, India. Huro: &nbsp; Also known as the Western Hoolock Gibbons. They are one of the species of apes which can be found in India. Nono: &nbsp; &nbsp; A Garo word for little sister Chigring: &nbsp; A traditional musical instrument of the Garos. See page 5. Kotip: &nbsp; A traditional headband worn by the Garo people. See page 6. Mongma: &nbsp; A Garo word for elephant.

Pagipa: &nbsp; A Garo word for father Magipa: &nbsp; A Garo word for mother Ambi: &nbsp; A Garo word for grandmother Michu: &nbsp; A Garo fried rice dish. Techri: &nbsp; A flower that blooms in the Garo Hills. Simsang: The name of a river that runs in the Garo Hills. It starts from the Nokrek Range and flows into Bangladesh.

Write A Letter To Huro! &nbsp; Huros love to read letters from new friends. Why don’t you write and draw a letter for a huro? &nbsp; Tell them your name, your favourite song, and your favourite food. You can also tell them a story. They love to read a story! &nbsp; Write your letter in the language you are comfortable in. Once you finish, put the letter in an envelope and give it to your teacher. &nbsp; Ask them to send it to the huros staying at the Huro Programme. Who knows! If the huros are not busy singing, they may write back to you!

# Rain

Rain rain come down in mighty torrents

and sticks to break

For villages to ring with delight and joy

For rice and grain to be sold a little cheaper.

Rain rain come down in mighty torrents

and sticks to break

For rice and grain to be sold a little cheaper.

About ‘Rain’ This rhyme beautifully captures the nostalgic essence of growing up in a place where the elements become part of cherished memories. The clouds are a part and parcel of people’s regular lives in the hills and with that, rain and thunder are inevitable. The author recalls how while playing in the rain and getting wet was a joyful activity, the rain also kept people inside to avoid catching colds. And there would be stern warnings from elders about the wrath of thunder and lightning.&nbsp; Rhymes and stories often serve as comforting companions, bringing joy even in the midst of gloominess and dullness, and the contributors to this book intend to share this comfort with those reading it.

'Rain,' is part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative by the Sauramandala Foundation. This book is a collaboration between the MLCU (Martin Luther Christian University) Shillong, and the TFFP.&nbsp; The TFFP team is grateful to Careen J Langstieh for sharing and helping&nbsp; document this “almost-forgotten” rhyme.

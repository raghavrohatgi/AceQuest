# Shampoo Day

It is Sunday. Shampoo Day. Rima likes to shampoo her hair. But she does not like what happens BEFORE that. Or AFTER that.

Rima eats breakfast. Two idlis and sambar.&nbsp; A glass of milk. She hopes Amma will forget what day it is. No luck.

“SHAMPOOOOOO DAYYYYY!” says Amma.&nbsp; Amma massages coconut oil on Rima’s head. She combs Rima’s hair.

Rima smells like chutney. Chicken curry.&nbsp; Aviyal.&nbsp; Pappadam.&nbsp; Fish fry.&nbsp; She is HUNGRY.

But Amma’s rule is ONE HOUR. One hour of coconut oil. It is yucky. Icky. Slicky. Slimy.

Rima is slipping in the oil. She is sliding across the hall. She is a baby seal.&nbsp; A slipping, slippery baby seal.&nbsp; She is sliding across the kitchen.

She is a baby hippo.&nbsp; A slipping, slippery baby hippo.&nbsp; She is sliding across the bedroom. She is a baby buffalo.&nbsp; A slipping, slippery baby buffalo.&nbsp; She is sliding across the balcony.

ONE HOUR!&nbsp; At last.&nbsp; Rima can shampoo her hair.

She smells of berries and flowers.&nbsp; Like a garden, but also a bakery.&nbsp; She wants to shampoo her hair forever. But…

There is a knock on the door.&nbsp; She hopes Achan is not waiting for her. No luck.

Achan wraps a towel around her head. Like a turban.

Rima runs.&nbsp; She is not a slippery baby seal,&nbsp; a slippery baby hippo,&nbsp; or a slippery baby buffalo.&nbsp; Rima is fast. Achan runs, too.

Across the hall, the kitchen,

the bedroom, the balcony. “Rima! We have to dry your hair,” says Achan.

“NOT ANY MORE!” says Rima.

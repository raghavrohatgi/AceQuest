# Nana and I

My Nana and I have such fun, us two, But there are many things we just can’t do. My Nana and I have such fun, us two, But there are many things we just can’t do.

I can’t stretch to the top shelf to reach the sugar jar,

He can’t bend to the bottom shelf for his chocolate bar.

I can’t swallow spinach without a scowl, He can’t eat ice cream without a howl.

His old TV remote I just cannot use, My new mobile game makes him blow a fuse.

I can’t climb easily onto his tall dining chair,

He can’t crawl into my tent without gasping for air.

I can’t name all the capitals of Indian states, He can’t remember important history dates.

I cannot drive a car, not yet anyhow,

He cannot balance well on his bicycle, now.

Sometimes I forget to brush my teeth at night, Sometimes, his medicines just disappear from sight.

He can’t see much without his glasses, I can’t see anything with his glasses.

There are many more things each one can’t do, But we can do one fun thing together, us two.

I can read a little. He can read a lot. I love Nana to read me a book that he’s bought.

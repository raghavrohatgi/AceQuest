# A Moon Full of Ants

I opened my eyes and stretched&nbsp; Poked a cloud by mistake&nbsp; Wiped my finger on a field And I made a great lake.

The lake thought&nbsp; it was mine and&nbsp; Followed me all around&nbsp; With seven shoes on its feet.&nbsp; What a terrible sound!

The best person in the world&nbsp; Called to ask me what’s for lunch. Thirty-seven ice-cream scoops&nbsp; Bowl of autumn—extra crunch.

I turned into a crow&nbsp; And won The Loudest Race.&nbsp; My father was so proud&nbsp; He grew a second face. The rabbit who lives on the moon Said I’m her best friend.&nbsp; She invited me to dance…

the ring-ding disco bend

The moon is full of ants&nbsp; So I won’t want to go.&nbsp; The unicorn who lives&nbsp; In my teeth told me so.

These are the sorts of things that Unicorns know.&nbsp; They used to be accountants Many moons ago.

I went instead to the park&nbsp; In the middle of the sea.&nbsp; The corals were delighted;&nbsp; They’d been so scared without me.

I told my banana joke— It’s true and also funny.&nbsp; The sun cried puddles laughing And it stopped being so sunny

The puddles led to a planet&nbsp; With toads and teapots in a maze.&nbsp; The sun forgot to set, so now

We’ve been there for plenty-one days.

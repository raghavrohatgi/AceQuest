# Pari's Polio Ravivar

Sonam and her niece Pari are playing with Khadu* . Sonam’s sister Seema is making amodi* . Krishna Bua comes to her house. ‘Tomorrow we are organizing Polio Ravivar* in the Public Health Center (PHC). Sonam, come with Pari for her polio drops,’ says Krishna Bua. Seema looks worried. *Khadu: Mountain sheep *Amodi: Unripe mango’s vegetable *Ravivar: Sunday

‘I don’t want to give Pari any polio drops. Last month she got a fever after taking drops,’ says Seema. ‘Fever is a side-effect of the drops. It protects the child from polio disease,’ says Krishna Bua. ‘I trust you Bua so I will come tomorrow,’ says Seema.

Krishna Bua went to other houses. Many people complained that the PHC was far from their house. They were right, first they needed help to reach help.

Lalu Chacha was alert after the wedding incident. He advised villagers to take care of their health. He also gave lift to mothers and children in the truck. This time many villagers came with their children to the Polio Camp outside PHC. Krishna Bua was happy to see so many children. There were five ASHA&nbsp;didis* from five villages and some Panchayat members. *ASHA Didis: Primary health activists in Indian communities

She was helping the nurse with distribution of iron and calcium medicines. Seema took Pari to the polio drops stand. ASHA Didi is giving the drops. Seema looked worried. Bua took Pari and helped the other ASHA Didi .&nbsp; Seema looked away.&nbsp; Pari cried after having the drop but stopped after some time. ‘Don’t worry Seema. My children have also taken drop,’ said Bua.

‘This is good for Pari but I was scared. Thank you for coming with me Bua,’ said Seema. Krishna Bua smiled and helped other children get their drops.&nbsp; Children were happy to get shahtoot* after the drops. *Shahtoot: Mulberry

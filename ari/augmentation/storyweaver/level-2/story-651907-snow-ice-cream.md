# Snow Ice Cream

Snow ice cream, Snow ice- cream,&nbsp; Dolma’s awaited winter’s dream.&nbsp; She hops around, under the blue&nbsp; sky,&nbsp; Chasing butterflies around the marigold&nbsp; fields,&nbsp; In her mind all she could think was&nbsp; snow&nbsp;ice cream.

The fire crackles, warm and bright, The kitchen is engulfed in its golden light. Dolma sits on a murah,&nbsp; with her chin on her hand.&nbsp; Watching Ama shaping the yeri with care, As Apa sips Sol-Chya, brown and bland.

She glances at the rack,&nbsp; where the steel cup lays,&nbsp; A cup of snow ice cream for&nbsp; the winter days. “Snow ice- cream, snow ice- cream! Much better than the broth,&nbsp; at least for me,&nbsp; Snow ice cream,&nbsp; that's the dream.”

Dolma and her chizo cleared the kateyra,&nbsp; Collecting the firewood, creating a space to gather for warmth.&nbsp; From a distance, continuous moos and the barks echo.

Lhamu aize screams, “ Lu hai chito garum hai! hiw parnu atyo .”&nbsp; The villagers quickly moved to collect hay and straw,&nbsp; Signalling the coming of&nbsp; the snow.

With her tummy full and facing the wall, Dolma counts the days until snowfall “Ama, it is going to snow tomorrow, right?” “Maybe, Nani,” Ama replies.

“Dolma! Dolma! Come out quickly!” Brush-Wash-Dress,

Wearing layers upon layers she takes flight. Outside the door, it’s her favourite sight!

Everything is covered up, all in white!

“Yangchen, Lakpa!” Dolma runs towards them.&nbsp; Chi- Nhi- Sum, Chi- Nhi- Sum.&nbsp; Rolling balls of snow and throwing them around,&nbsp; They all jumped, making the crunch crunch sound.

Dolma scooped up the snow into her steel cup.&nbsp; Her cheeks flushed red as she gobbled it up. “Aiya! My teeth hurt, my teeth hurt,” Dolma screams.&nbsp; “Mine too,” her friends laugh, gulping down their own&nbsp; snow ice-cream.&nbsp; One scoop, two scoop, three scoop – four,&nbsp; Until Ama stopped them from eating more.

Now, with their tummy full of ice,&nbsp; Holding up the cup, they all smiled.

Glossary ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Murah- A small wooden tool to sit ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Sol-chya- Butter tea or tea ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Yeri- Yeri also called as Lumde which is the traditional dish of Sherpa people made &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;with wheat– based dough balls cooked in different flavourful soup. ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Chizo - Elder brother ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Kateyra - a place where people keep fire wood ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Aize - Elder sister ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Chi – Nhi- Sum, Chi- Nhi-Sum – One – two- three- four ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Aiya - Ouch! ●&nbsp; &nbsp; Lu hai chito garum hai! Hiw parnu atyo - Let us do it quickly. It is about to snow.

About the Story This poem is a mirror reflecting the childhood memories of the author, who grew up experiencing snowfall in her home village of Sangdara in Uttarey, Sikkim. For the communities living in this small valley, especially the people of the Sherpa community, snowfall was considered a blessing from God and was a source of joy for both children and adults. The author focuses on a special memory of hers where she eagerly awaited snowfall so that she could enjoy her own snow ice cream. Not knowing what real ice cream tasted like, for the author, the taste of freshly fallen snow in winter was a much awaited delight. However, with climate change altering the way of life, such snow-filled winters have become a rarity for the people&nbsp; of Uttarey. Through this poem the author wishes to preserve the stories and memories that the&nbsp; people of Uttarey associated with the snow and, through that, celebrate the rich&nbsp; cultural tapestry of the communities in Uttarey.

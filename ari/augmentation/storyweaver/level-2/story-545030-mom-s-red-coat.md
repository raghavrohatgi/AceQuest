# Mom's Red Coat

I love Mom’s coat! It’s red and long and warm. And it smells like Mom!

I love doing things with Mom! We picnic outside. She hugs me when I come home from school. We work in the garden. At night, she holds me tight until I fall asleep.

‘Good news!’ says Mom. ‘I found a job! I will be away all week, but I’ll come home on weekends!’

‘Who will hold me at night, Mom?’ ‘Who will hug me, picnic with me, garden with me?’

‘I can see that you are sad. I will leave my red coat here with you.’ ‘When you see it, remember that I love you and I’m coming back.’

I drag the red coat outside – it’s my picnic blanket! It feels like Mom is with me. Oops! Juice spill!

I am sad that Mom’s not waiting at the door when I come home. I put on her coat. It’s warm and it smells like her. It feels like a hug! Oops! Paint!

I help Gogo in the garden. It feels like Mom is gardening with us. The long coat on the branch scares away the chickens! Oops! Mud!

When it’s dark, I miss Mom most. Gogo covers me with her warm coat. It feels like Mom is holding me as I fall asleep. Oops! Cookie&nbsp; crumbs!

Mom’s red coat is warm and smells like… Oops! We need to wash it! paint, juice, mud and cookie crumbs!

The wind blows the red coat off the washing line... up into the air! It flies over the fence! Oops! Who did the coat fall on? It flies over the taxi!

‘Found you, coat!’ The red coat smells like Mom again. I run and grab it! ‘Oh!’ ‘Found you, Mom! You’re back!’

# Happy Hands

Lana Iba Sam Janai Tori Miss Lari

Today’s art class is in Miss Lari’s pottery studio in Larnai.

“Wow! I want to try some pottery too, Miss Lari,” says Lana.

“Today we will learn how the black clay from the Sung V alley helps make these beautiful black pottery pieces.”

“Look, the clay is soft!” says Tori. “Did you add black paint to it?” asks Lana. “I have never seen black clay before,” says Iba.

“These are so beautiful,” says Janai. Miss Lari smiles at the excited children.&nbsp; “Yes, they are beautiful indeed, aren’t they?”&nbsp;

“How did the clay harden, Miss Lari?” asks Lana. “Where can we find the black clay?” asks Sam.

Miss Lari tells them that the the black clay is dug out from the Sung Valley. A serpentine stone is crushed until it turns into a green powder.

The women then mix the green powder with the clay and it is&nbsp; sticky! &nbsp; They form different shapes with the sticky clay and then leave them to dry in the sun.

The dry pottery pieces are dipped into the dye made from the bark of Sohliya trees.

The hardened pottery pieces are then sold. People come from far away places to buy them.

“How come we don’t see these pottery pieces in the shops near my house?” asks Janai “Maybe because Larnai is too far and not many know about it,” Lana answers him.

“Miss Lari, look at my bowl!” says Iba

“What do you think of Larnai pottery?”

Activity Ready to make your own pottery piece?&nbsp; Using simple ingredients found in your kitchen, you can create different shapes! 2 cups of flour 1 cup salt 2 cups water 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil 1. Mix all ingredients into a dough. 2. Enjoy making your own hand pottery piece. 3. When the piece is dry, you can colour it with water colours.

Glossary Sung Valley: A valley that has the most fertile soil in Jaiñtia Hills. Sohliya tree: Bayberry tree.

'Happy Hands,' is part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative of the Sauramandala Foundation.&nbsp; The TFFP team is grateful to Ms. Roilin Pyrtuh of Larnai Village for sharing &nbsp; extensive information on the process of pottery making, without which this book would not have been possible.

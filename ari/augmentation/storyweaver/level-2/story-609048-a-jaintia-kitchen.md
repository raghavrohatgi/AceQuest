# A Jaiñtia Kitchen

TPAI DI Ñ &nbsp;A traditional fireplace where food is cooked.

&nbsp; THAW PNŪCHO Ŋ KCHU A hollow tin used in a fireplace to make pots stable and prevent them from shaking.

CHIAP Used during fishing to store fishes that are caught; some people also use it for smoking fish and meat.

KCHU UMSUM An aluminum pot used for boiling water for baths.

KCHU JA An aluminum pot used for cooking rice.

KCHU JHUR An aluminum pot used for cooking curry.&nbsp;&nbsp;

KHNĀM A contraption used for catching fishes from paddy fields or rivers.

KHOH A basket made of bamboo designed for multiple uses – transporting rice, firewood, clothes, etc.

THLO Ŋ &nbsp; A traditional pestle and mortar.

SBE SARU Corn stored for its seeds which are then used for sowing. &nbsp;

MURA A traditional bamboo/cane stool used for sitting.

‘A Jaiñtia Kitchen,’ is one of the three books which is part of 'The Kitchen Series.' 'The Kitchen Series,'&nbsp; is part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative by the Sauramandala Foundation. The TFFP team is grateful to the families and individuals of Shnongpdeng village and Laskein village for opening their homes and sharing about the different types of kitchen items used by the people of the Jaiñtia community. Without them, this book would not have been possible.

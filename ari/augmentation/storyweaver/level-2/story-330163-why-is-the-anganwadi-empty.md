# Why Is the Anganwadi Empty?

Rita is the Anganwadi*&nbsp; teacher for her village. *Anganwadi: Rural child care center for children between age 3 and 6

The children have left the&nbsp; Anganwadi Center. She is marking the attendance record.&nbsp; There are twenty young children in the village. Only five students attend regularly.&nbsp; Rita is worried. Rita has her next meeting in two weeks at the Rishikesh office.&nbsp; She needs to convince parents before that.&nbsp; She also needs to make tikka cards* for these children. *Tikka cards: Vaccination card

She visits children who did not take admission. All parents give different reasons for their children not coming. Some say other children of the village will fight with their children. Some don’t want their children to eat at Anganwadi .&nbsp; Some grand-parents want to teach their grand-children at home. Some say their children will cry when they are away from their mother. And many are not at home, but in the city. Only two parents agree to send their kids from tomorrow. At the end of the day, Rita is tired and goes home.

The next day, Seema comes to pick up Pari from Anganwadi .&nbsp; ‘Why are you upset?’ asks Seema. Rita tells her everything.&nbsp; ‘I have an idea that can help,’ says Seema. Rita looks at Seema curiously.

In the evening Seema goes with other parents to convince the villagers. Sonam, Chutki, Pinku and Anuj go with her. She takes Pari’s notebook and drawing books. Other parents also help her. They all go to different houses. Seema goes to Pinku’s house. Pinku’s Dadi doesn’t want to send Pinku’s little sister Mahi to Anganwadi . She says that Pinku’s mother can teach the children at home. Mahi cries every time she leaves home.

‘The children can learn at home but going to Anganwadi &nbsp;they will make new friends,’ says Seema.&nbsp; ‘Yes, I have so much fun in school,’ says Pinku. ‘Dadi she will cry for a few days because she is not used to leaving her mother but it will help her,’ says Seema.&nbsp; ‘Okay Seema I trust you. We will send Mahi to Anganwadi tomorrow,’ says Dadi.&nbsp; This way parents convince villagers to send their children. Rita thanks Seema and other parents.

Next day there are ten more students in class.&nbsp; Many children were crying but Rita knows they will stop crying after a week. Rita starts making all the children’s tikka cards. She is happy that the children of the village will be more healthy and safe now.

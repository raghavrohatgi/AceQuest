# Shapes on the Shore

“Don’t go out in the dark. There are strange things on the seashore,” warned Thatha.

“Don’t go out in the dark. There are strange things on the seashore,” warned Thatha. So Varun anna, Yashika, Hari, and I sat on the verandah of Thatha’s house and played a game: “Can you guess what is on the shore?”

“Maybe they are mangroves,” I said. “Magical trees whose roots point to the sky!” “Maybe they are mangroves,” I said. “Magical trees whose roots point to the sky!”

“Beaches are made of fish poo, aren’t they? They look like very big poos left by very big fish,” said Yashika. “Beaches are made of fish poo, aren’t they? They look like very big poos left by very big fish,” said Yashika.

“I think they’re sandcastles,” said Varun anna. “It’s a sandcastle city for tiny little crabs.”

“Maybe they are sea monsters that have come to enjoy the big beautiful moon,” said Hari. “Maybe they are sea monsters that have come to enjoy the big beautiful moon,” said Hari.

Then I yawned, and so did Yashika, Varun anna and Hari. “Good night,” we said to the strange things on the shore. Then I yawned, and so did Yashika, Varun anna, and Hari. “Good night,” we said to the strange things on the shore.

At dawn, we could not wait to discover who had won the game.

None of us had won. The strange things were only piles of garbage that had followed us all the way from the city. None of us had won. The strange things were only piles of garbage that had&nbsp; followed us all the way from the city.

I hope they follow us back too. I hope that they follow us back too.

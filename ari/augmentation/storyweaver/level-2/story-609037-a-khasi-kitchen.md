# A Khasi Kitchen

LYNGWIAR DPEI A traditional fireplace used for cooking and heating water.

RYNIENG / TYNGIER A place used to store or dry logs of wood or any other bamboo-like items.

JINGTHANGDOH Used for smoking meat above the fireplace.

KETLI Used for boiling water or making tea.

KHIEW PHIANG Aluminum pots used for storing water.

DABOR Mostly used for washing vegetables and washing utensils. Sometimes it is also used for storing water.

RIEWHADEM Corn stored for its seeds which are then used for sowing.

TROP Used for carrying farming produce.

PRAH Traditional bamboo tray

KHASI ALMARI Traditional Khasi cupboard.

DULI Traditional Khasi cabinet.

SHAWLA Traditional Charcoal heater.

‘A Khasi Kitchen,’ is one of the three books which is part of 'The Kitchen Series.' 'The Kitchen Series,' is&nbsp; part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative by the Sauramandala Foundation. The TFFP team is grateful to the families and individuals of Madanlyngkhi village for opening their homes and sharing about the different types of kitchen items used by the people of the Khasi community. Without them, this book would not have been possible.

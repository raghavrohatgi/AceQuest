# Red Rice

“Bei!!! I’m so huuuunnnggrryyy!!” “Oh, &nbsp; Khon ! You’re home! Look at your clothes! Go clean up. &nbsp; I’ve just put the rice on,” Bei replies warmly.

"Bei, Bei, guess what! I scored a goal today! I shot it from waaayyy back, and it went right into the net! Everyone &nbsp; clapped so hard," Dap exclaims with &nbsp; a smug &nbsp; smile. “Awww, congratulations, " &nbsp; Bei smiles. “Ah, it's red rice! Why have you cooked red rice today, Bei?” asks Dap.

“Oh! &nbsp; Since you have your football practice tomorrow, the red rice will give you the strength that you need,” says Bei. " But how? " Dap asks.

“Well, firstly, red rice is rich in fibre and proteins, which makes you strong and is good for your tummy. And the second reason is……a secret!” “A secret? Tell me, please!” Dap eagerly asks.

Bei tells him, “Well, red rice is very special to us, khon . We work tirelessly &nbsp; to get the fields ready because red rice grows best in well-prepared soil.”

“And when the field is ready, they are then sown into each of the muddy beds that &nbsp; have been prepared for them. You have seen Kong do it, haven’t you?” asks Bei.

“And as you know, water is like magic for plants, especially for rice! People would &nbsp; consistently keep the water in check.”

“Then we wait four to six months to harvest the rice. That is when the &nbsp; 'rashi' and 'khoh' are used to collect and separate the rice from the plants. It's like a big harvest party for the rice!"

“Since everyone in the village puts so much effort into making it, our red rice gives back strength to us!” “Ready to eat? The rice is all done!” exclaims Bei with a smile.

&nbsp; “ Thank you, Bei!” says Dap, taking in the aroma of the hot red rice. “Mmmmmmm! Thank you, red rice! I’m going to score more goals tomorrow! "

Activity &nbsp; Separate the red rice from the husks. You will need: A small amount of red rice or white rice Bowl/Chalani Clean Hands

Take a bowl or winnowing tray. Pour red rice on it. You can gently rub the rice grains together to remove husks. Pick out any loose husks. Repeat until most of the husks are removed. Your rice is clean and ready to cook!

Glossary Bei: A Pnar word for mother. Khon: A Pnar word used to lovingly call young children. &nbsp; Rashi: A sickle used for harvesting paddy. &nbsp; Khoh: A basket made of bamboo designed for multiple uses – transporting rice, firewood, clothes, etc. Chalani: &nbsp; A winnowing tray.

# Sun Mother

In the months of Mangshir and Poush , dark clouds cover the skies. The cold winter is here. Two sisters, Dolmo and Palmo, shiver from the cold. But the sun does not show. *Mangshir and Poush are the names of months on the Nepali calendar.

Shine on, oh Sun Mother. Your baby cow is sick in the cold.

Shine on, oh Sun Mother. Your calf trembles in the cold.

Shine on, oh Sun Mother. Your kid trembles in the cold.

Shine on, oh Sun Mother. Your puppies whine in the cold.

Shine on, oh Sun Mother. Your chicks wail for some warmth.

Shine on, oh Sun Mother. Your Dolmo and Palmo are in danger in this cold. Oh look! Sun Mother listens to us. She shines for us. She pushes away the dark clouds.

Sun Mother is a song sung by the children of the Tamang community in winter. The lyrics are inspired from the original song but written in a new form as a nursery rhyme. The sun is referred as a mother who gives warmth and protects all living beings

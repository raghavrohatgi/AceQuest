# Melody March

As the melody maiden’s &nbsp;tune begins to play, People from near and&nbsp; far come her way, Happiness spreads,&nbsp; and sadness goes away.

I’m the Illep, short and neat, With my pep-pep-pep-peeeeee ,&nbsp; I can't be beat, Children come close, tapping their feet.

Behold! I am the Otekra! long and thin, I sing tweee-tweeee-tooo in the wind, Like a bird's song,&nbsp; making children grin.

Step aside! I’m Gongmina, small and free, My twaing-twaing-ta-twaing &nbsp;is the best, you'll agree! People’s biggest smiles? They’re all for me.

Look here! I’m Sarenda, curvy and fun, With my weee-weee-woooo , I shine under&nbsp; the sun, When I play, everyone knows joy has begun.

Hah! They call me Dama, mighty and bold, I make a sound, ding dading dimita so old, Feet tap along, as my rhythm takes hold.

Make way! I am Dotrong,&nbsp; the wooden one, My strum-strum , so full of fun, Perfect for a day under the bright sun.

Top of the charts? Dimchrang, I say, My twigs go dim-chrang dim-chrang ,&nbsp; children sway and sway.&nbsp; Every moment’s a delight,&nbsp; come what may.

With every note we help the&nbsp; music maiden play, Together we brighten everyone's day, All worries and troubles just fade away!

Activity Match the instrument to the sound! 1. Ding-Dading-Dimita 2. Dim-Chrang Dim-Chrang 3. Tweee- Tweeee- Tooo 4.&nbsp; Weee-Weee-Wooo 5.&nbsp; Twaing- Twaing- Ta-Twaing

Glossary Illep: A short and smaller flute traditionally played by young lovers to express their feelings. Otekra: A long and thin flute played during the Wangala festival. Gongmina: A small Garo musical instrument, often made of bamboo or metal. Sarenda: A string instrument similar to a violin, played with folk songs. Dama: The traditional drum of the Garo people, commonly played during the Wangala festival. Dotrong: A string instrument resembling a guitar, often played during joyous gatherings. Dimchrang: An instrument made of re or rattan. It is played alongside Cheraa , which are Garo stories told in the form of songs.

# I would have liked it if...

You know, I like many things. But Aai doesn’t like some&nbsp;of them. Sometimes Baba does not like&nbsp;some of them. Or nobody likes anything— Aai, Baba, Aajoba or Aaji!

See, Aai doesn’t like flowers. I would have liked it if she did. But…

“Megh, are you going to go to school wearing this?” says Aai. “Yes,” I reply. “It will be OK.&nbsp;My teacher will not shout at me.&nbsp;Sayali always comes to school&nbsp;with flowers in her hair.”

Sometimes it gets very boring at&nbsp; Aaji&nbsp; and Aajoba’s place. Aajoba always wants&nbsp; to play cards. But it is not fun. I would have liked to play with Cookie. But…

"Nooo! Aaji, hold Cookie!" “Don’t be scared, sona,” says Aajoba. “He will not do anything.”

Okay, forget about Cookie. I would have really liked it if Aaji let me eat anything I want. But…

“Aaji, I am hungry. I want to eat something small. Something gooey . Maybe something chocolate-coloured.” “Hmm, shall I give you some dates?” says Aaji.

What if every day were Diwali? I would have really loved it! But…

“Spinach? Aai, please make shrikhand with chapatis?” “Shrikhand–chapati!” says Aai. “Every day is not Diwali.”

I would have loved to sleep alone in my own room. In fact, Baba would have loved it even more.

Actually, no. This is so much better.

# When a Forest Wakes Up

From behind the stony backs of snoring hillocks, The sun jumps out, roaring into a bright morning.

The air hums and flutters, dancing around in circles. As the sun walks the land, the soil twists and churns colours.

Trees whisper old secrets to each other, through millions of buried messengers. Branches open their mouths wide, to swallow the sunlight.

Then the sun hides behind a herd of giant grey clouds, Who trumpet and shower, filling the trees with water.

As the wind sweeps the clouds clear, the water flows downward, a jolly river.

By evening, a painted sky soars high. Birds fly back to flowers to thank them for their colours.

A dark, sleepy night perches on the sun. The forest prepares for a new show, a new day.

And floating by the horizon, the sun, never too far away.

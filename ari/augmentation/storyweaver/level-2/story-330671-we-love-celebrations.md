# We Love Celebrations!

Chutki, Pinku, Anuj and Sonam are taking a walk in the village. They look at the water tanks and solar plants at every house. ‘Our villages have changed so much in this year,’ says Pinku. ‘Yes, now we have electricity and water tanks,’ says Chutki. ‘Many students are going to Anganwadi* . Everyone is using the new pakka road* ,’ says Anuj. ‘We have solved many problems,’ says Sonam. *Anganwadi: Rural child care center for children between age 3 and 6 *Pakka road: Concrete road

‘Do you think we should celebrate?’ asks Pinku. ‘We love celebrations!’ they said with excitement. ‘Every year we have a thaula to welcome the spring months. We can celebrate these new changes this year,’ says Sonam. The children decide that this year’s Baisakh Thaula* will be bigger. *Baisakh Thaula: Spring fair

In the coming days, everyone starts preparing for the thaula* . The adults decide to bring homemade items. Lalu Chacha is helping to get things from the city by making rounds in his truck. *Thaula: Fair

On the day of the thaula , people from nearby villages visit. The village is decorated with flags and spring flowers. Everyone is singing to the tune of Bedu pako, bara masa* ... as they walk to the Thaula . *Bedu pako, bara masa: Folk song of Uttarakhand

Sarpanch* is selling arsas*&nbsp; with Charu Chachi. Lalu Chacha is going to eat many but Pinku comes and stops him. ‘We cannot have one more emergency,' says Pinku, taking the arsas away from Lalu Chacha. Lalu Chacha looks sad. Charu Chachi starts laughing and doesn't give him more arsas . Krishna Bua is selling the Garhwali special kode ki roti* with makhan* and chutney* . Aashi Madam and Sonam are enjoying the yummy roti . *Sarpanch: Electoral head of the local governance body of a village *Arsa: Arsa is a sweet Pahari delicacy that is prepared on special occasions like weddings and traditional celebrations *Kode ki roti: Finger millet chapati *Makhan: Butter *Chutney: Thick sauce made from vegetables or fruits

Akbar Bhai and Salim Bhai are selling toys. There are dolls, cars, mobile phones and tiny drums for children. Chutki gets a mobile phone for herself. Seema and Rita Madam have made jhangora ki kheer*&nbsp; and jalebis* . Pari and Mahi love the kheer* so much that they finish the entire bowl. Dadi has made baskets and lamps from bamboo sticks and bhimal* . The people from other villages love her baskets. She has already sold twenty of them. *Jhangora ki kheer: Barnyard millet pudding *Jalebi: A sweet snack *Kheer: Rice pudding *Bhimal: Grewia tree

In the evening the Sarpanch is telling everyone about the teamwork of the village. The Sarpanch thanks everyone for bringing together this lively mela* . After the speech, Rakesh and Rajesh play the dhol* and masak baja* . The villagers dance in celebration. Chutki, Pinku, Anuj and Sonam are very proud of their village. *Mela: Fair *Dhol: Dhol is the traditional instrument which is used in almost every occasion in Garhwal *Masak Baja: Masak baja is a traditional musical instrument from Uttarakhand played in special occasions

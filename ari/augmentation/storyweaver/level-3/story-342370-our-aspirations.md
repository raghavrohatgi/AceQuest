# Our Aspirations

It is a sunny day. Chutki is going to school in Lalu Chacha’s truck. Pinku is trekking up the same hill. ‘Thudddd!’ falls Pinku, slipping on the gobar .&nbsp; ‘Hahahaha’ laughs Chutki. Pinku frowns at Chutki. She always laughs at him.&nbsp; He goes to Anuj’s house to clean the gobar .

‘What happened?’ Anuj asks. ‘I slipped on some gobar ,’ replies Pinku. ‘But why are you angry?’ ‘Chutki saw me slipping, she will tell it to the entire class now.’&nbsp; ‘Don’t worry, I won’t laugh. I will also talk to Chutki about this,’ said Anuj.&nbsp; 'Thank you Anuj you’re very kind,’ said Pinku smiling at his friend. 'Now let’s go to school we are late,’ said Anuj picking up his bag.&nbsp; They stop at Charu Chachi’s little shop to buy tiny toffees.

When they reach the bridge, goats block their way.&nbsp; Pinku plucks some mangoes from the huge mango tree. ‘Nom Nom!’&nbsp; They say eating the juicy mangoes.&nbsp; The goats have left. Now they are more late to school.&nbsp; They run from one side of the bridge to another.

Finally! They reach their school in Lakshpur.&nbsp; They slowly enter their class and sit behind everyone.&nbsp; On the blackboard Pinku sees a big English word, ‘A-S-P-I-R-A-T-I-O-N-S.’&nbsp; Pinku scratches his head and wonders what does it mean?

Aashi Madam asks, ‘What do you want to do when you grow up?’&nbsp; ‘I want to write poems for children!’ says Chutki. ‘I want a big shop in Rishikesh,’ says Pinku proudly.

‘I want to be a Gram Pradhan and and take care of my village,’ says Sonam. ‘I want to be a tour guide and tell people about the beautiful places,’ says Anuj.&nbsp; ‘Wow! All of you have different aspirations,’ says Aashi Madam.&nbsp; ‘Madamji so this ‘aaz-peee-ra-tionns’ are dreams?’ asks Anuj.

‘Correct, Anuj. Now let’s go outside,’ says Aashi Madam.&nbsp; ‘Yayy!’ exclaimed the kids.&nbsp; In the middle of the school ground there was Pali Peepal. They all stand under the tree. ‘Do you think trees have dreams?’ she asked them. ‘No, how can trees have dreams they can’t think,’ said Chutki.&nbsp; ‘Maybe Pali Peepal wants to cover the entire school courtyard with its shadow,’ said Pinku.&nbsp; ‘Do you think it can achieve this dream?’ asks Aashi Madam.&nbsp; ‘Yes, it can,’ said Sonam.

‘What will it need to grow tall?’ ‘Sunlight, rain and soil,’ replied the children.&nbsp; ‘Hmm… is that all?’ asked Aashi Madam.&nbsp; Chutki looked up at the big tree and saw a sparrow’s nest.&nbsp; ‘Madamji it will also need birds!’ she exclaimed.&nbsp; ‘Very good Chutki, what will the birds do?’&nbsp; ‘They will dance, play and sing songs for the tree,’ said Sonam.&nbsp; ‘What do we learn about Pali Peepal?’ asks Aashi Madam.&nbsp; ‘Pali Peepal needs sun, rain, soil and birds to achieve its dreams’ said Chutki smiling.

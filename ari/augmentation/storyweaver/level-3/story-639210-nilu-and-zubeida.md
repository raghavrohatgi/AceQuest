# Nilu and Zubeida

Zubeida’s blue friend is nowhere to be seen, and it is the very last day of the mela! Tomorrow, Nilu leaves for another country. It is their last meeting of the season. Has Nilu left without meeting her Zubi? How could she do that!

Zubi pulls out the red ribbon from her braid and waves it in the air, again and again. Her neck hurts from looking up at the sky.&nbsp; But the sky is empty.&nbsp; Oh it’s blue, but without the brilliant blue&nbsp;of her Nilu. With heavy steps, Zubi walks towards the mela.

Yellow jalebis, crisp imarti, new golgappa flavours, mouth-watering rabdi and kulfi, and Nilu’s absolute favourite—corn, and, of course, popcorn for Zubi! They had planned to gobble it all. Then they would play the new, melodious flute and hum together tons of tunes. But now what? The silly-billy has left her alone! Zubi’s tears are bitter with rage and she’s lost all taste for sweets.

The mela is bursting with colours—ruby red, oh-so-bright orange, blackest black, shimmering golden yellow. What’s missing is a brilliant blue to tease all the other colours. &nbsp;

The Giant Wheel is turning. Zubi’s stomach is churning, with worry. Nilu is her best friend, so why hasn’t she come? Is she anxious about going away? Did Nilu’s uncle stop her from making friends with humans? Or did a hunter… oh no! Zubi gets off the Giant Wheel and runs. She wraps corn in a leaf and buys two red lac bangles. A bangle for her and a bangle for Nilu.

The semal tree is just ahead,&nbsp; quiet&nbsp;and lonely.&nbsp; The frogs are croaking merrily in&nbsp; the&nbsp; stream, “Finally, the blue-winged nuisances have scooted!” Under the semal, she digs a small hole and buries one bangle. The other bangle she leaves by the river. “Two friends never love each other equally. One loves more, the other less. I love more, Nilu loves less. I don’t want such a selfish friend!” Zubi curses them. She stuffs all the corn into her mouth and then spits out half.&nbsp;

At home, Dadi asks, “Did you eat something at the mela, Zubeida?” Zubi wipes her tears, rubs her nose and darts straight into her room. She flies, like Nilu, and falls on her bed, so no one can see her tears and her swollen face. The pillow turns into a lake of Zubi’s tears. She lifts her nose. Nilu is sitting at the window! She is holding something red in her beak. She has come for Zubi.

Zubi leaps, Nilu flies! The red ribbon in her mouth, Nilu chases Zubi for one last game. In the pale blue sky, a brilliant&nbsp;blue, a long dangling line of such a red hue, and on the ground, laughing,&nbsp; Zubi yoo-hoo.

Zubi jumps and flies and bounces! She doesn’t let go of the ribbon . They fly over villages, eat wild berries, bop dogs on their heads, scatter the hay from haystacks, chase grasshoppers, and stitch some clouds together.

Zubi freezes. The blue kite becomes small. She pulls in the string and rolls it up, but the kite has&nbsp;its own mind…

Who knows, maybe Nilu will come again next year to celebrate the summer holidays with Zubi?

AINDRI C I am Aindri and I think bats are upside down hearts! Let’s make a nature print of a fruit &nbsp;bat. First, we go on a shape hunt. Then we draw, mix, paint!

Nature printing is a very old method of capturing impressions of plants with a variety of mediums. They make for excellent shape studies. You can press fallen flowers into a book!

The yellow-red- blue&nbsp; colour palette&nbsp; when&nbsp; mixed offers us a&nbsp; range of warm and&nbsp; cool colours. Don’t be afraid to get muddy!

My name is Priya. I like telling stories through pictures. I love trees, crows and cats, and I love watching people go about their daily lives. I don’t think there is ‘one’ way to get new ideas for making things, but it helps if you observe the world around you very carefully. It always starts with a thought. I don’t exactly know where the thought&nbsp;lies, but it feels like the thought radiates&nbsp;from somewhere in the back of my head. I think a better way to describe this would be a galaxy of thoughts. Because some things shine bright and clear like the stars. Other things seem fuzzy like gas. And some things are just dark matter. But it helps to draw these thoughts out, even though they don’t make complete sense. PRIYA KURIYAN

These thoughts follow me as I do the dishes… When I do my daily errands… Or when I watch the world as it passes me by. I give it some time. I revisit my old drawing. And slowly things grow clearer. People often describe me as a creator or a maker, but I think...

I am just a borrower.

# Sweet Sunday

“Go to Manjit Paaji’s shop and buy any two things that each of you likes,” she says. The siblings are thrilled and after lunch, they set out for the sweet shop. It’s the first day of the holidays. Maa calls Simran, Jeet and Veer and tells them she has a treat planned for them.

Simran chooses phirni,&nbsp; Jeet chooses toshe and&nbsp; Veer wants jalebi.

Manjit Paaji weighs the sweets. The children watch as he measures and packs them in boxes. He says that the&nbsp; jalebi weighs 200&nbsp; gms,&nbsp; the phirni weighs 250 gms&nbsp; and&nbsp; the toshe is 500 gms.

“Ma said we are allowed two things each,” says Simran. Simran chooses samosa. Jeet chooses mathri and Veer takes kachori.

This time, Manjit Paaji &nbsp; &nbsp;weighs the salty stuff. He tells the children that &nbsp; &nbsp;the samosas weigh 500 gms, &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;the mathri is also 500 gms and &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;the kachoris weigh 300 gms.

They run home and Ma asks them what they have bought.

The children say that each of them has bought a different mix of sweet and salty treats. Jeet’s treats weigh a kilo, Simran’s weigh 750 gms and Veer’s weigh 500 gms. Everyone gets busy enjoying the feast. The holidays have begun well!

1 kg = 1,000 gms 500 gms + 500 gms = 1,000 gms = 1 kg 250 gms + 500 gms = 750 gms = 0.75 kg 200 gms + 300 gms = 500 gms = 0.5 kg

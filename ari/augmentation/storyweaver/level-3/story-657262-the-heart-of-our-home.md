# The Heart of our Home

"The smell of pithas made during every Bihu season in our kitchen takes me back to many memories." Biswajit Thakuria Assamese, Assam

"No matter how busy we are throughout the day, dinner is the one meal we share together as a family in the kitchen.” Chorovelu Ringa Chakhesang &amp; Angami, Nagaland

"The first thing I do when I come home is to enter the kitchen and the last thing I do when I leave home is to enter the kitchen. Kitchen always reassures me of the warmth of my family." H.A Sothing Shimray Tangkhul Tribe, Manipur

“Our kitchen is the heart of our home; its hearth brings warmth to our family.” Longang Luklem Konyak Tribe, Nagaland

Rildong serves the warmth of broth, enriching the flavour of the rich heritage of the Himalayas. Best known for making “Sisnu and Dehro.” Tashi Chong Sherpa Sherpa Tribe, Sikkim

“Kitchens are where life is shared with loved ones and bonds are formed with strangers.” Kemya Yanlem Konyak Tribe and Tangkhul Tribe, Nagaland

“These kitchens are a source of a lot of good memories for me.” Moge Basar Galo Tribe, Arunachal Pradesh “The smoky aroma of Rabko, combined with the scent of Ane’s (Mother’s) daily cooking and her presence in the kitchen every early morning, is something I always long for.” Marba Kamki Galo Tribe, Arunachal Pradesh

“My kitchen is my happy place, I feel happy when I cook, when I peel, and when I hear the sizzling sounds.” Chalini Basumatary Bodo Tribe, Assam

“While most kitchens back home have now transitioned to a modern model, my earliest memory of going back to my village is of sitting by the Sauka (the earthen hearth) and waiting for the elders to make Sunga Pitha, a way of roasting rice inside hollow bamboo tubes.” Kasturi Dev Chaudhury Assamese, Assam

“I remember the hymns, the stories, the groundnuts roasting in the khadai and the presence of my loved ones in cold winter evenings. The fireplace knows it all.” Niboli K Chophimi Sumi-Naga Tribe, Nagaland

"The kitchen is the heart of the home. In a Mizo household, the 'tapchhak' connects food, love, and memories. Home-cooking, especially traditional recipes, is the ultimate expression of love." Joan Lalramngaihsangi Mizo Tribe, Mizoram

“A friend once asked me where one should go in Manipur to truly experience the essence of the place. I told them, a kitchen in Manipur. Our kitchens are sacred spaces. Each community in Manipur has its own unique setting, but as a Meitei, no matter how modern our amenities become, we always dedicate a spot for Phunga Lairu, the hearth, where we believe our Indigenous deity, Emoinu, the goddess of wealth and prosperity, resides. For me, as a passionate cook, my kitchen is not just a place to prepare food, it’s my lab, a space where we gather as a family, discuss important matters, and ensure that it’s always stocked with essentials. The most important of them all? Ngari, the fermented fish, a true staple in our kitchen.” Potsangbam Linthoingambi Chanu Meitei Tribe, Manipur

“Home is where my cat is, and my cat is always in the kitchen staring at the smoked meat hung by the fireplace.” Keneisenuo Solo Angami Tribe and Tangkhul Tribe, Nagaland

“In the comforting bamboo house kitchen, where ashes drift and earthy scent uplift, I hear echoes of my childhood – Flames of mud oven infused with the aroma of food that shaped my earliest tastes, a space - where I first read and learned; where my earliest joys and achievements found their voice.” Thomas Malsom Hrangkhawl Tribe and Molsom Tribe, Tripura

# Myntdu

Long ago, in a land far away, Where laughter echoed throughout the day; Water sparkled, twinkled, and flowed, In a village blessed, a magical abode.

From Bay of Bengal, the nymph made her way, Delighted by the village's charm, she chose to stay; Sipping 'Umchaŋpet ,' where waters play, In nature's embrace, forever to lay.

In Mihmyntdu, a cherished stream, Myntdu River, like a serene dream; 'Ka tawiar - takan,' &nbsp;by the village's side, Helping when troubled, a comforting guide.

Long ago, when conflict brought a chill, An&nbsp; epidemic ' Ka Khlam-Ka dait' &nbsp;gave a mighty&nbsp; thrill;&nbsp; But Myntdu's magic, a healing song, Helped the village, made them strong. &nbsp;

In Mihmyntdu's tale, a challenge arose, An illness spreading, worries it sows; Mon, an elderly figure by the riverside,&nbsp; Awoke at dawn with sharp insight.

To the river's embrace, he went near, Egg, basket, and rice, his symbols clear — The ritual's whisper, like the comforting song; "Meet me in Muchai," hope is growing strong.

By the river so wide and majestic, A man called out with hopeful feat; "Ghost or Nymph, hear my call, If you're not well, come, let's talk!”

Surprise! A Nymph popped up,&nbsp; oh so kind,&nbsp; Told the man, "Choŋ-sah, you are welcome to stay,&nbsp; but keep this in mind,&nbsp; No washing stuff in me,&nbsp; or throwing things away;&nbsp; Keep the promise every five years,&nbsp; to keep the illness at bay. "

" Clean the village, keep water so clear, No sickness will come, nothing to fear. " A promise made, simple and sweet, A happy village, where all can meet.

From Myntdu river, where stories became, Mihmyntdu, their village name; Through meadows and hills, where dreams embraced, Mihmyntdu thrived, love and river interlaced.

Glossary Umchaŋpet: It refers to spring water. Myntdu: A river in the Jaiñtia Hills. Mihmyntdu: It is a small village in the Jaiñtia Hills, Meghalaya, and is named after the Myntdu river. Ka Tawiar Ka Takan : It means 'guardian angel.' Ka Khlam - Ka Dait: It means plague or disease. Mon: A Pnar word for 'will.' It is also the name of the old man who performs the rituals. Muchai: The first day of the week in the Pnar calendar. Choŋ-sah: It means ‘stay. ’

# Pommy and Tommy

Mummy loved dogs. Nani loved cats.

Piya loved dogs and cats. Baba had 13 cats when he was growing up!

But Pommy was scared of dogs and cats. And all animals.

Pommy and her family had just moved to Hyderabad from Kolkata. She was away from Nani, Nana and everyone she loved. She had no friends. Pommy would cycle around her apartment building alone all evening.

“Pommy, come here!” Baba said one day. “Look at that dog. He looks hungry. Do you want to feed him?” Annoyed, Pommy dragged her feet all the way to the balcony but...

Pommy had never been captivated by a dog before. He had big brown eyes, a soft brown-and-white coat and a bushy tail. Their eyes locked, and time stopped. “WOW!”

Baba and Pommy made friends with the dog. The neighbours called him Tommy. Tommy loved biscuits, roti and dal-rice. “Pommy is in love with Tommy,” Piya would tease. Pommy did not mind. Tommy, too, had eyes only for Pommy.

One day, Tommy was sitting under his favourite tree. Just then, the sky turned grey. One flash of lightning, one rumble of thunder and down came the rain! Pommy and Piya squealed, running for cover into their building. “Where are you going, Tommy? You will get wet. Come back!” screamed Pommy as she saw Tommy run away.

Soon enough, Tommy was back. “There! Good boy. I was worried — Oh! Why are you pulling me? What is the problem?” said Pommy. Tommy led Pommy and Piya to the end of the lane. There, Tommy signalled at a pair of glowing eyes.

“Oh no! There are kittens stuck in the drain,” Piya said. Pushing aside her fear, and out of love for her brave Tommy, Pommy bent down and quickly scooped out the kittens. Piya covered them with her skirt.

“Look, Mummy, look who Tommy helped save,” said Pommy, pointing at Piya. Mummy took the kittens in her arms. It seemed that Pommy and Piya became invisible to Mummy as she cooed over the kittens. “Where is their mummy? She must be looking for them,” she suddenly said.

As soon as the rain stopped, Mummy, Pommy and Piya, each with a kitten in her hands, and with Tommy trotting next to them, asked around for a mother cat. But she was nowhere to be found.

So Mummy packed the kittens, Piya and Pommy into an autorickshaw. They rushed to the nearest doctor for animals, a veterinarian.

That was the first time Pommy had set foot in an animal rescue shelter. There were dogs, puppies and cats everywhere. There were some rescued goats and pigs, too.

Dr Akhil Bandhu checked the kittens and smiled. “They are healthy, and their temperature is normal. Nothing to worry about. But they are too young to survive in the shelter without their mother. Could you foster them for a while and then get them adopted?”

“We will take care of them. Let us take care of them,” Piya and Pommy pleaded while Mummy called Baba. Baba remembered his 13 cats and agreed. “You girls have rescued them, now it’s your duty to find them new families.” “Please, Mummy, please!”

Pommy’s neighbour aunty Shreya agreed to adopt two kittens. Baba and Mummy agreed to adopt the remaining one, much to Pommy’s joy.

A new world had opened up for Pommy. There were so many animals who needed love and some company. She now knew how she was going to spend her summer holidays: at the animal shelter! What amazed Pommy was that the animals seemed friendlier, too. “Were they always this friendly and cute?” she wondered.

Tommy was the hero of the locality. Aunty Shreya had named her two kittens Jason and Meowgi. Pommy wanted to name her kitten, too. “I want to name her Pushkin!” Mummy said. Pushkin came sprinting to Pommy, her eyes glowing in the dark. Pommy planted a small kiss on Pushkin’s forehead. Animals no longer scared her.

She was in love, again.

# The Big Beach Clean-Up

Arzu loved the beach more than&nbsp; any&nbsp; other place in the world.

There, she could watch her&nbsp; favourite animal,

play her&nbsp;favourite game

and have her&nbsp; favourite drink,&nbsp; coconut water.

She would go as often as she could with&nbsp; her best friend, Umeed. One afternoon, they&nbsp; found Thatha decorating his coconut stall. “Thatha, which beach is this?” asked Arzu,&nbsp; pointing at a photograph. “It’s so clean and beautiful!” said Umeed.

Thatha told them how he grew up on&nbsp; this very beach. He would play barefoot&nbsp; and take naps on the sand without having&nbsp; to worry about pieces of glass or garbage. “It was heaven! It pains me that you will&nbsp; never see it like that.” &nbsp;

The next day in school, Arzu told her entire class about Thatha’s old photos and&nbsp; stories. She described how beautiful the&nbsp; beach once was. “We have to do something!&nbsp; And I have a plan.” Everyone wanted to help!

Arzu showed them the map she had made. She had drawn the whole beach and&nbsp; divided it into four equal parts or quarters. The plan was to divide themselves into four&nbsp; equal teams. Since there were 16 children in the class,&nbsp; this meant that each team would have four &nbsp;children. Each team would collect and&nbsp; separate garbage from one quarter of the&nbsp; beach.

At sunset, Abu arrived as promised with&nbsp; the garbage truck. He weighed all the&nbsp; sacks before loading them.

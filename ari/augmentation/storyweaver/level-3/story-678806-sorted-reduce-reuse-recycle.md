# Sorted!: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Let’s start sorting all this waste!

Here are 5 ways to manage waste: REFUSE Buy only what is necessary. REDUCE Why replace when you can repair? REUSE Repurpose materials for multiple uses: metal containers for storage, old fabric for cloth bags, or inks and refills for pens. RECYCLE Process materials back to their original form: plastic from plastic buckets can become plastic buckets again. RECOVER Turn food, garden or farm waste to compost. Convert wood, contaminated textiles, non-recyclable plastics, etc., into new energy by burning them in incinerators to replace fossil fuels as a source of energy.

ALL SORTED! Waste is all around us. It is made of materials that were loaned to us, which we can use to make other things. We can grow food in soil enriched by compost made from kitchen waste. We can reduce how much new material we extract from the earth by sending materials to factories to recycle. When not properly disposed of, waste can neither be used by factories nor put back into the earth.&nbsp; It sits there, making us unhealthy, becoming more and more difficult to reuse. It poisons the environment. But when treated as a resource, waste is not waste at all. The illustrations in the book are made using organic waste, old denim and newspapers and even broken pieces of aluminum.

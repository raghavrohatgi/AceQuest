# The Guardians of Bodha

A long time ago, there was a village called Bodha. It was surrounded by a beautiful dense forest. The villagers got everything they needed from the forest. An old man named Gautham was the headman of the village.

One day, three greedy woodcutters came from the neighbouring village. They wanted to cut the trees of the forest to become rich. They convinced the youth of the village by saying, “If these trees are cut down, you can make big buildings here. Your village can become a city. We have cleared the forests of all neighbouring villages and helped to transform their villages to cities.”

But when they were about to cut the first tree, Gautham stopped them. He said, “I will not let you cut a single tree. If you protect them, they will help you when needed.” The youth agreed and sent the woodcutters back.

Some days later, there was heavy rain. It flooded all other villages except Bodha. The people of those flooded villages ran to Bodha for shelter. Gautham asked the Bodha villagers to give them help.

The three greedy woodcutters were also there. They had also lost their homes. They asked Gautham, “All other villages are flooded! Why is Bodha not flooded?” Gautham answered, “These dense forests guarded us from the flood.”

They made a promise at that moment. “We will not cut trees and for each tree that we have cut, we will plant two saplings.”

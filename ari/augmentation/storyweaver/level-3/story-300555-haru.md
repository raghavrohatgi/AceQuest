# Haru

Hi, I’m Haru!

This is where I spend most mornings. My favourite breakfast is spicy&nbsp; chickpea curry, buttered toast with&nbsp; sugar on top, and sweet, milky tea.

On hot days,&nbsp; I can find shade.

On rainy days, shelter.

On cold nights, warmth.

One day, I got hurt on the busy road.

Dida found me.

She took me home.

She took care of me. She fed me vegetables and fruit.

My new family is nice. The boys are in trouble today so Dida is making them knit scarves.

My leg is fine now. Here I am, helping with the laundry.

Itʼs not a bad life.

Haru was a real goat! He was rescued by Granny Ituko in Santiniketan in West Bengal, during the 1970s. He grew up as a member of her family, along with many dogs, birds, cows, squirrels, pigeons and ducks, and one snake. Goats are intelligent and funny, and they come running when called, especially if you learn to bleat like them — which I did. They can be really mischievous, they chew on almost everything, they get into all sorts of trouble! This is how I imagine Haru’s life was.

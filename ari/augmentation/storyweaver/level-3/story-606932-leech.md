# Leech!

There were only two things that Shanbor enjoyed doing– bossing around and lazing away. “Yaaaawwnnn!! &nbsp; Another day to watch over my people working!”

While he loved bossing around, he enjoyed lazing around even more. So lazy was he that he didn't even want to go to his paddy field to supervise his workers. "Let me take a peek through my window."

“Are they even working? &nbsp;I can’t&nbsp; see.&nbsp; Yaawnnn. I don't want to go and check now.&nbsp; Maybe tomorrow.”

"Maybe tomorrow," he said.&nbsp; &nbsp;And he said this everyday!

Then one fine day, Shanbor thought of a BRILLIANT idea! “Aha! I just know what to do! I am so so smart!”

“Hello Ding Ding and Mon Mon!&nbsp; I have an important job for you two.”

“Go watch over my fields and workers for me.&nbsp; Report everything to me in the evening. Don’t be late!”

As Ding Ding and Mon Mon left,&nbsp; Shanbor went back to sleep. “ Now I can go back to sleep.&nbsp; Ding Ding and Mon Mon will do all the work. They will be back in the evening.”

As Shanbor snored away to glory, Ding Ding and Mon Mon began their adventures. “Ding Ding, let’s go look for the tallest tree to climb!!”

“Mon Mon, this one here is the tallest!” “No, Ding Ding, that one over there is the tallest!”

"Adididi! &nbsp;Adadada!&nbsp; Aninini!&nbsp; Hurry Mon Mon, I can't keep an eye on your side of things!"

“We have to be diligent and report everything to Master!”

“M-mm-master, we are back.” “Ding Ding! Mon Mon! What took you both so long?&nbsp; Report everything to your Master, now!” “Yes, yes Master!”

Pleased that he can sleep all day, everyday, Shanbor puts Ding Ding and Mon Mon back. “Time to get back inside,&nbsp; Mon Mon, Ding Ding!”

The same cycle continued everyday.

One fine day as Mon Mon and Ding Ding were watching over their Master’s fields…

…a crow comes and swoops them up… “ Masterrrrrrrrr…!! HELLLPPP!!”

…and flies away!

As the sun went down and it got darker, Shanbor started wondering where Ding Ding and Mon Mon were. “I can’t see anything! &nbsp; Why are Ding Ding and Mon Mon not back??”

A few days passed by and Shanbor managed to crawl out of his house into the dark night. So deep into the night it was that everyone was shut up in their houses. There was no one to help him! “Water! Water!&nbsp; Somebody give me some water!”

“Thirsty... I am so thirsty!&nbsp; Maybe I can quench my thirst if I suck my own blood.”

“Somebody!&nbsp; Anybody!&nbsp; Deliver me from this misery!!”

It seemed like someone had heard Shanbor’s plea. &nbsp; He was turned into... &nbsp;a LEECH!

Activity Shanbor is thirsty!&nbsp; Could you guide Shanbor to the water source to quench his thirst?

Glossary &nbsp; &nbsp; Ding Ding: The name of the left eye in the story. In standard khasi, 'left' is kadiang . The nongkrem variety is kading , hence the name Ding Ding . Mon Mon: The name of the right eye in the story. Kamon is the khasi for 'right,' hence the name Mon Mon .&nbsp; Adididi! Adadada! Aninini!: A Khasi expression or sound used when one is tired or exasperated.

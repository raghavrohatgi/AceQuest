# Passang's First Syabru

As the hills came into view, Passang could barely control his excitement. After eight long months in Gangtok, he was finally back home in Naghi.

“Gaga tikpey!” Passang ran towards his grandmother. “Hahaha, Angha lebuja!” his grandmother said,&nbsp; giving him a big hug. “Come inside, little one.”

“Ah, Passang-La!”&nbsp; Pachey Dawa called out. “You are home for the Losar. It has been so long.” “Lai Azangla! Tashi Delek!” Passang greeted everyone.

“It is your first Losar in Naghi, right? You can finally perform the Syabru with everyone,” Pachey Dawa said. “Yes, yes, I am so excited!” Passang said, eyes bright. “I must say, Passang, I can’t wait to see how well the grandson of the best Syabru dancer performs,” said Pachey Dawa to Pasang.&nbsp; “Gaga is the best? Really?”

“Really,” Azang Nima joined in. “In fact, Losar in Naghi is incomplete without her Syabru. People from other villages attend our Losar celebration just to see her perform Syabru.”&nbsp; “Your Sano Gaga is the most beautiful dancer in Naghi. Everyone will come to watch. You must make her proud,” Pachey Dawa said, while crunching the khabsey. Passang felt his hands&nbsp; go cold, even though he&nbsp; was&nbsp; holding a warm cup of tea.&nbsp; Back at the hostel, he had practised a little during the open traditional dance competition. But now, sitting in front of everyone, he knew that it would never be enough.

“Will everyone really come?”&nbsp; Passang gingerly asked. “Yes, of course! Let us see if you can keep up with your Gaga,” Azang Nima adds. Everyone laughs, but the knot in Passang’s stomach twists further.

By the time dinner was over, Passang’s heart pounded like a drum.&nbsp; He could already picture the crowd, all eyes on him, standing next to his Gaga.&nbsp; She would be beautiful while he would clumsily stumble around.&nbsp; Would they all laugh at him?&nbsp; Gaga would be so disappointed. He could not do this.

“Passang! Passang?” Gaga’s voice broke him out of his thoughts. “Yes, Gaga?” “Here you go, little one.”&nbsp;&nbsp; Passang looked at the beautiful fabric she handed to him.&nbsp; “This is your chubba. You will wear it tomorrow for Syabru,” she said with a smile.

The uncomfortable knot intensified in his stomach. “What is it, Angha?” “Lai Gaga, everyone is expecting me to perform the Syabru, but…&nbsp; but I don’t think I can do it,” Passang softly replied. “Oh dear, why is that?”

“Everyone says you are the most beautiful dancer and that they think I should be the best dancer as well.&nbsp; But I have never danced Syabru before.&nbsp; What if I get confused and&nbsp; mess up the&nbsp; steps and&nbsp; then embarrass you?&nbsp; It is&nbsp; better if&nbsp; I don’t perform it at all.” He couldn’t hold back his tears. “Oh, come here.” Gaga gives him a hug.

“Pachey’s words really worried you, didn’t they?” Passang nodded with a small sniffle. “You can never embarrass me, darling, especially not when dancing the Syabru. Remember, little one, Syabru is not about the steps. It is about coming together and celebrating our culture. Do not worry about doing it perfectly,” Gaga said.

“But what if I do mess up the steps?” Passang asked. “Then just feel the movement of the person next to you,”&nbsp; she explained. “Every movement has meaning. Holding hands together shows our shared community bond. Stamping our feet echoes the strength of the earth beneath us. The circular formations remind us that we are all connected.&nbsp; If you feel that in your heart, you don’t have to worry about anything else.”&nbsp; Passang was deep in thought. “If you really don’t want to dance, then I will not force you,” Gaga gently said, “but Syabru is about being together and preserving our traditions. Everyone should be a part of that. Besides, I want to dance with you on your first Syabru. Wouldn’t you like that?”&nbsp; Passang finally smiled. “Lai Gaga, I really would!”

The next day, Naghi was filled with laughter and cheer. Everyone was dressed to celebrate Losar. “Losar La Tashi Delek, Angha!” Gaga greeted Passang as she adjusted the folds of his chubba. “Losar La Tashi Delek, Gaga,” Passang replied.

Passang watched as a big circle of women, children, and elders began to move in perfect unison. "Nyungma Kapu, Nyungma Kapu… Kepi Kelu Mashe Nga…! “Are you ready, little one?” Gaga gently asked. “Yes.”

Drup! Drup! Drup! Aley Lungshu, Aley Lungshu. Shabdo Yetang Yenla Dinlajyok.

Ya Shupi Metok Lenzeamu. “Gaga, I am doing it! I can do it!”

“Well done, Passang!” Everyone cheered. “He performs just like his Gaga!” Gaga beamed. “See, Nani! You are part of it now.” “I can feel the connection, Gaga!” Passang joyfully cheered. “ Losar La Tashi Delek! ”

Passang was the star of the celebration. After the celebrations, he walked home, hand in hand with Sano Gaga, humming to the tune of Syabru.&nbsp; He would never forget this Losar celebration.

Glossary • “Angha lehbuja!”: “Oh little one, you came” • La!: A respectful way to say ‘Yes’ • Pachey: A Sherpa word for big uncle • Azang: A Sherpa word for uncle • Deshok: “Come here” • Kuchen: an expensive Buddhist printed cloth • “Dhi khang Hing?”: “What is this?” • Angha: A Sherpa word for small kid • Tashi Delek: Greetings

About the story The story brings out the essence of traditional folk dance, Syabru. The dance of connection, gratitude, and fun, Syabru holds a deep cultural significance among the Sherpa community. The story “ Passang’s First Syabru ” highlights the importance of preserving and passing down the culture and tradition, especially to the younger generations as they learn and grow in this contemporary world. This story is about finding the meaning, learning and connecting with the roots and traditions in order to keep it alive.

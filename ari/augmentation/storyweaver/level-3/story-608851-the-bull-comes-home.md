# The Bull Comes Home

Beneath August's warm gaze…..&nbsp; Bram’s hands hold a jackfruit leaf, folding and unfolding until… &nbsp; &nbsp;…. a creature comes to life.

A leafy bull awakens.&nbsp; It locks eyes with the young boy, and they share a soft smile.&nbsp; “I’m home,” the bull thinks.

The creature becomes Bram’s little friend.&nbsp; Floating in leaf boats during Bram's bath, it watches the clouds pass slowly by.&nbsp; And listens as Bram shares his dreams.

The bull meets Bram’s friends.&nbsp; They tell their own tales using jackfruit leaves…&nbsp; Windmill blades dance in the wind, birds take flight in dreams and boats sail imaginary seas.

Bram and his friends create and share joy, while the bull quietly watches..

As the days roll by, the children grow older.&nbsp; Laughter of the playgrounds fades away.&nbsp; The leafy friends start to disappear… one by one.

The windmill quietly twirls away in October.&nbsp; Dreaming of distant skies, the birds fly off next. &nbsp; And then, the boat follows…

By December, dust settles on the bull in the corner of Bram’s room.&nbsp; Hopeful, it waits, as Bram enters. He only picks up a cardigan and leaves again.

The bull goes out to the jackfruit tree where it was born. It waits.. and waits.. but Bram doesn’t come.&nbsp; The bull grows tired, its eyes drooping. “I’m so sleepy….” it murmurs.&nbsp; And so it curls up under the tree and sleeps.. and sleeps..

Seasons change, and in its slumber, the bull becomes something new and fresh —&nbsp; a jackfruit leaf falling gently into waiting hands.

A child gazes at the green leaf, tracing its delicate patterns. Her hands start to fold and unfold, following a secret map hidden in the leaf.&nbsp; Slowly, a familiar shape appears — the bull, born again.

The child skips over to Atchu * Bram with the bull in hand.&nbsp; Bram's eyes twinkle with recognition. He traces the familiar grooves and patterns with his fingertips. * Atchu: A Garo word for grandfather.

The bull locks eyes with the old man, who was once a young boy.&nbsp; Delighted, it lets out a heartfelt sigh and says,&nbsp; “I’m home."

Activity&nbsp; Hey, there!&nbsp; Ready to create something magical?&nbsp; Let's make our own special bull using a jackfruit leaf. Just follow the steps given below. 1. Take a jackfruit leaf. 2. Make cuts along the first row of veins on each side to make the horns. Then, cut off the end leaving the middle vein intact for the tail. 3. Fold and tuck each side under the horns.

4. Flip the leaf to the other side and gently fold one side on top of the other. 5. Pin the two folded sides with a small twig or toothpick. 6. Make a small hole below the horns and insert the stalk in it. Ta-da!&nbsp; Your leafy bull is complete.

About 'The Bull' 'The Bull,' is the signature piece of designer Arak M Sangma, who has illustrated this book. The creature holds a special place in his memories. He fondly recalls a happy time from his youth, crafting a bull with his friends under a jackfruit tree. Through his art, he aims to bring back that joyful feeling, honoring the special moments spent with friends in the lap of nature. Crafting bulls and other shapes from jackfruit leaves is a cherished part of childhood for many who grew up in the Garo Hills. It's a core memory, a thread woven through people's past, connecting them to the joy and laughter of those early days.&nbsp; The artist has infused personal touches from his own memories into the illustrations — wisps of smoke connecting the pages, a toy cart, and a shelter made of banana leaves.

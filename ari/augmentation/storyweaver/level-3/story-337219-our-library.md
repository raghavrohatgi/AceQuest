# Our Library

Every morning, when the library door opens…

Ishaan whooshes, to his corner at the far end of the library, to find out what trees say to each other.

Marriam, with her car collection, looks through the latest magazines. She wants to find the fastest sports car ever and become a race car driver. Aabir loves picture books without words. Unlike words, pictures don’t jump, slip, hop and dance.

Divya, her Amma and Nanna make small towers of books. The towers tumble. A book falls open. That’s the one they read. Sneha and Abdul bhai have been building a reading table to hold her books on magic, to help her prepare for the magic show at school.

In the afternoons, the library fills up with laughter, chatter and the jingle of toys. It’s story time! Today, everyone is reading about the adventures of a boy and his Nani.

Amrita and Kumail love to arrange all the books, and mend books in the book hospital. They want to be librarians someday. Parmeet always brings his box of magnifying glasses, and the question, “Why do books have such small writing?”

Kabir loves the book his Amma made using photographs, sand and pebbles from their last visit to the beach. Two years ago, Haya khala and Pramila ben met in the picture book section. Now they are best friends and make books for children who read with their fingers.

Ryan and Priyanka didi have made a quiet den in a dark corner where Ryan reads with a torch. On the days when Jill is feeling sad or angry, she spends all her time in the library watching the light dance on the pages.

Every Saturday evening, the Book Club of the Disabled Youth meets. The library smells of homemade food, and is filled with the sound of laughter and chatter. Would you like to join us? Bhaiya is going to read us a story now.

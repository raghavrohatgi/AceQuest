# Goplu's Train Ride

​Every Wednesday, my Thammi picks me up from school. Thammi says that trains are the fastest way to travel in this city.

​Thammi smiles at everyone. Even strangers. She says if you smile at people, they smile back.

Sunil Uncle is Thammi’s friend. He always gives me a toffee through the holes in the grille.

Trains have grilles too. I put each finger in one square and hold on tight as the train sets off.

​Inside the train, aunties do all kinds of things. They cut vegetables.They knit sweaters.

​They put on make-up. Some even sleep, sitting up straight.

​There seem to be fewer people on our side of the grille. More and more aunties get into the other side.

I see a little girl on the other side of the grille. Will she be my friend? I give her a shy smile…

​…And she smiles back! Thammi was right. I turn to tell Thammi about my new smile friend.

​But Thammi is busy. She’s taking out my snack from a box. Today, there’s luchi-jam roll. My favourite.

I turn to show it to my friend. She has a packet of chips. We smile as she passes me one tiny chip.

​Thammi tears a piece of the luchi-jam roll. But it doesn’t go through the squares of the grille.

Our station is here. I am sad that I could not share anything with my new friend. Just then, Thammi taps my pocket. I remember Sunil uncle’s gift.

​I fish out my favourite toffee… And I quickly give it to my friend. She smiles. I smile back and get off the train.

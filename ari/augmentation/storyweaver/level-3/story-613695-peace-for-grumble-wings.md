# Peace for Grumble Wings

Grumble Wings was a grumbling moth. He grumbled about darkness, lemons, and even mints on tablecloths. He grumbled about sunshine and the rain. He grumbled again and again and again.

One day, as he grumbled around a light bulb, Wind came so fiercely that it made him gulp! “Uggh! Oouff! Poof!” * he grumbled as he liked to do. “Where are you taking me, Wind?” he cried “To make you stop grumbling,” she replied. *“Uggh! Oouff! Poof!”- These are funny sounds Grumble Wings makes when he grumbles.

Wind stopped at a village on top of a hill. It was quiet, peaceful, and so very still. “Uggh! Oouff! Poof! Who lives here anyway?” Grumble asked with a frown. Wind smiled and said, “Little silk moths in pretty white gowns.” “A moth is a moth! Uggh! Oouff! Poof! I don’t need a lesson from one of my kind, I like to grumble and I’ve made up my mind. Take me home now, Wind! It's been a rough day. I’ll never stop grumbling no matter what they say! "

Just as Grumble Wings was grumbling aloud, A little blond moth flew up to him and bowed. “Hello! I’m Eri,” she said rather softly. “I’ve heard of your grumbles and I’d like to tell you my story.” “She’s a silk moth,” said Wind with a smile. “Smile, relax, listen, we’ve flown so many miles.”

“I don’t have time for stories or songs,” said Grumble Wings with the ugliest frown. “I don’t like friends or pretty moths in white gowns. And why are you smiling, little Miss Moth? Wind brought me here against my will. She blew me across twenty odd hills!”

“I’m Eri,” repeated the little moth with a smile. “Oh, do hear my story, it'll only take a while.” As she pleaded, she danced around the lamplight, Wind promised Grumble if he listened she’d take him home that night. “Uggh! Oouff! Poof!” Huffed Grumble Wings as he rolled his eyes. “Fine! Lets hear your story. I'm sure they're all lies!”

But when I hatched and a worm I became,&nbsp; Stretching and crawling were my favourite games.” &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;

" They put me in a little basket covered with leaves so green. Juicy, yummy Castor leaves * fit for a Queen. The leaves were my bed, the leaves were my food… " “Excuse me,” interrupted Grumble, “but that is just rude! You don’t eat your bed, you don’t sleep on your food! " *Castor leaves: These are the leaves of the castor plant that silk worms love to eat.

“Oh Grumble! Grumble!” laughed little Eri, “Please just listen to my story. Castor leaves are a silkworm's favourite food. It gives us strength, it keeps us in a happy mood. It’s the weavers who keep us in these baskets you see. With no wings to fly, little worms just can’t flee. So we sleep on the leaves and we eat them too. For what else can a little silkworm do?”

Grumble made a face as he started his grumble. He rolled his eyes and began to mumble. “But is that all you do all life long? Eat, sleep, and potty? That just sounds so wrong. No wonder you don’t grumble, you’re just plain lazy. Living like that would drive me crazy!”

Eri giggled with a little nod “That’s our little secret but you know what odd? Eating Castor leaves turns us green and yellow and fat And after 20 days we spin a cocoon just like that!” Eri giggled with a little nod. “That’s our little secret&nbsp; but&nbsp; you know what's odd? Eating Castor leaves turns us&nbsp; green and yellow and fat. And after twenty days we spin&nbsp; a cocoon just like that!”

Grumble Wings laughed and said, “A cocoon is what all moths come from. Some call it ‘home’, others call it ‘Mum.’ Look at all these moths dancing to the light of the moon. Each one was a crawlie that could spin a cocoon.” He sneered as he said, “You were a silkworm, you say? Well, I heard all their cocoons are boiled every day! You must have escaped it, for here you are singing. I’d do too, sing each day without ever grumbling!

“I’m a silk worm!” Eri puffed her chest in pride. “There’s a difference so let me set the record right. I spin my cocoon with a door to get out.” “Uggh! Oouff! Poof! A door?” Grumble did shout. “Well, yes!” nodded Eri, “A little hole at the end of my cocoon. From which I came out like air from a balloon.” “No grumbles?” asked Wind, looking at Grumble. “No,” answered Eri, “not even a mumble.” There was no boiling me and no shouts. The weavers released me and took my cocoon. They collected empty cocoons and boiled those all afternoon.

They took them and boiled them in a very big pot. The cocoons turned to silk in the pot that was hot. The silk threads were then spun to a ball. One ball. Two balls. A hundred in all.” “It sounds like a lie,” said the grumbling moth. “I bet the weavers enjoyed silkworm broth! “Uggh! Oouff! Poof!”

“So the silk thread balls,” carried on Eri, ignoring Grumble to finish her story. “The weavers then weave them on their looms. Saris, shawls, jai ñ sems soon fill the room. They sing as they weave each silken thread. Each piece of clothing is spun with peace, it is said. Because, my little cocoon with peace did I make, And with peace I came out for peace’s sake.”

“Grumbling helps,” Grumble muttered unconvincingly. He liked her story and now knew his grumbling was a bit too silly. Eri smiled at Wind, then back at him. She liked this new Grumble who wasn’t so grim. So she continued her story from when the weavers made clothes.

“So the clothes dyed with colours are hung high above, On clotheslines that arch like rainbows of love. As they dry in the hot summer air, Each garment becomes a whispered prayer ."

" Then come the buyers from near and far. They come in buses, bikes and cars. To see, to buy what they’ve seen and heard. To touch what peace feels like, not just a word. Silk threads given as gifts to a world full of Grumble Wings. Grumble Wing insects and Grumble Wing Kings, Grumble Wing men and Grumble Wing women And even little coats for grumbling children. "

" So now do you see, Grumble Wings, Why I don’t grumble but only sing? When I’m happy inside, I am at peace. My silk threads are not just a home for me, They are my prayers to the world, my plea. That peace brings peace no matter how you do it. Either spin it or weave it or however you see fit.”

“I see now, sweet Eri, that I was wrong. You gave your home and then your silk. You are brave, generous, and oh so kind While I’ve been silly, mean, and blind. The world needs more peacemakers like you, sweet you. I may be a simple moth but I will be one too. So no more grumbling under my roof. From today, no more “Uggh! Oouff! Poof!”

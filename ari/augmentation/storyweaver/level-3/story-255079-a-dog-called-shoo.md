# A Dog Called Shoo

“Shoo!” cried a man on a bike. “Get out of my way.” “Shoo!” yelled a mother. “Stray dog, beta. Don’t touch it.” “Shoo!” shouted the owner of the coffee shop. “Don’t scare my customers away.”

The little brown dog shrank into a dark corner. Everybody always said, “Shoo!” So she began to think that was her name.

Shoo hadn’t always lived on the streets. She had once lived with a family. The boy threw terrible tantrums all the time.

“Show me a trick!” &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;“Bring me that stick!” &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; “Up on your legs!” But Shoo didn’t know how to perform any tricks. She was scared.

Soon, the boy got bored with Shoo. It was time for a new toy.

One day, the father left Shoo at the bus stand and drove away.&nbsp; Shoo waited and waited. But he never came back.

The dirt of the streets stuck to Shoo’s fur.&nbsp; Slowly, Shoo learnt to dig for food in the bins. First, it was hot and sticky.

Then, it was wet and chilly. Cold and hungry, Shoo lay by the rubbish heap.

One day, she spotted Adit walking down the street.&nbsp; He came closer and looked at Shoo. He pulled out a lunch box from his school bag.&nbsp; There were still some idlis left.

Every day after that, Adit would share his lunch with Shoo. In the beginning, Shoo was shy. She would wait for Adit to leave before she gobbled up her food. But soon, she began to jump up with a little yap when she heard the sound of Adit’s shoes. Thap, thap, thap.

One day, as Adit turned to go home, Shoo followed him. Every time he looked back, Shoo would hide behind a pushcart, a tree, a scooter. Adit stuck his hands in his pockets and whistled as he walked.

When Adit reached home, he said, “Papa, look, we have a guest! Can she stay with us?”

Shoo followed Adit everywhere. She sat outside the bathroom while Adit sang in the shower. When Adit went to school, she lay at the front door, waiting. Shoo worried. What if Adit went away? What if he never returned?

A year flew by. Shoo and Adit became best friends. Shoo understood that if Adit ever went out, he would always come back. Shoo had a new name now.

“Toffee, give me back my book!”

# Nyeke Monyu

I am Nyeke Monyu. A log drum from Pessao.

For centuries, I stood a mighty tree. Rooted deep, whispering with the wind, Drinking the rain, reaching for the sky. Waiting for my destined call. Then one day, a man came to me. “Mighty tree, you have been chosen to be our log drum. May you not resist or bring us difficult times. But instead, bring blessings to the land and the people.”

And they came. They came in large numbers. They came singing their songs. And I knew what was coming for me. It was the moment I was waiting for. The moment I was preparing for. As excited as I was, I was also nervous. I asked the wind to sing for me. To see me through the process.

With each stroke, with every chant, I felt myself changing. No branches to stretch, no leaves to sway. Was this what it took to become a log drum? Was this still me?

When they were finally done, They sang: “ Yeangyong maoba Lakma shah-ung tao pu. ” I looked like a completely different being.

Nine days they took to pull me from the jungle. When I reached the morung, the village rejoiced. Men, women, and children joined in celebration. Singing, dancing, feasting. In the morung, I found myself surrounded once again. They struck me, and I made a sound: Dang dang kon, dang dang kon. They were celebrating my arrival.

Slowly I began to feel at home. Slowly I understood the people. I had lost my roots but found a new stand. No leaves to sway, no branches to stretch, But my songs took flight.

I became the heart of the village. I became their voice. I learnt to laugh with them. I mourned with them in their sorrow. And I sang blessings upon the morung.&nbsp; I carried messages. Of victory, of mourning, of safety, or of warning and alert.&nbsp; I echoed them far and wide.

I have become a part of the village. And the villagers, my family. I have become- Their voice. Their witness. Their guardian.

Glossary Log drum: A musical instrument carved from a single tree Pessao: A Konyak village in Mon district of Nagaland “Yeangyong maoba, lakma shah-ung tao pu”: “The mighty tree, now a log drum” Morung: A traditional naga educational institution and dormitory for young men

About the story Log drum in an integral part of Konyak Naga villages. The log drum—carved from a single massive tree—is more than just an instrument; it is sacred. Traditionally kept in the morung (youth dormitory), the log drum serves as the voice of the village, calling people together in times of celebration, mourning, danger, or war. Its different beats and rhythm carry different meaning, echoing messages across the hills and forests. In this poetic narrative, the log drum of Pessao village speaks of her own journey and her transformation into the heart of the village.

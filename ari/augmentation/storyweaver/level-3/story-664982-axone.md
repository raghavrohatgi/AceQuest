# Axone

Do you know about Axone?

‘Axo’ means ‘smell’, and ‘ne’ comes from ‘thonhei’, which means ‘deep lingering’.&nbsp; Some say, ‘ne’ comes from ‘chine’ meaning grind/crush. Axone is a Sumi Naga delicacy enjoyed by all tribes of Nagaland.

I love axone with smoked pork. Imu loves axone chutney. Ipu prefers it with yam. Ifo likes it fried.

But all of this starts with axone pa and the magic in Iza’s hands. First, Iza takes the soybeans and soaks them in warm water. I love putting my hands inside this warm bowl.

Then Iza takes out the big-sized pressure cooker from the shelf to cook the beans. It whistles so many times!

I don’t like the fountain of brownish water that comes out of the cooker as it whistles. It needs a lot of cleaning later. Iza adjusts the flames a lot. Sometimes it's on full high heat, while sometimes it's on medium heat. Iza even leaves it to simmer.

Once the cooking is done, Iza drains the water, puts them in a bamboo basket,&nbsp; and covers it well with an old newspaper or leaves. She then closes it with a lid and keeps it in the corner of the fireplace for some days to ferment.

Now comes the time to pound! Before pounding, Iza gets ready with the banana leaves and washes them clean. She then warms the leaves from the fire so that they won’t tear when we wrap the axone. “Be careful with those; they can tear.” Iza always warns me.&nbsp; Of course&nbsp; I know that!

A thick-bottomed large pot and&nbsp; a large pestle will do. Imu is the best when it comes to pounding the axone. Thud! Thud! Thud!

After pounding, Iza scoops the paste out and transfers it to the banana leaves in our hands.&nbsp; We wrap them into rectangular shapes.

Ipu makes the best rectangular-shape axone pa among us. Ours is a little crooked.

Then axone pa is left above the fireplace for a few days.

And BOOM, there is Axone.

Iza cooks them with smoked pork. Ifo crushes them and fries them with raja mircha. Imu’s axone chutney is ready after he crushes the green chillies with axone. Ipu likes it with eggs too.&nbsp; And I like adding fried axone to my noodles. After all this work, we enjoy axone however we like.

Glossary Ipu: a sumi word for father Iza: a sumi word for mother Ifo: a sumi word for sister Imu: a sumi word for brother Axone pa: cake of axone Raja mircha: king chilli

About the story Axone, known for its distinctive flavour and aroma, is a staple in every Sumi household. It is a processed fermented food made of soybeans. It is loved across Nagaland, particularly among the Sumi tribe.&nbsp; Fun fact: Fried axone with raja mircha (king chilli) is packed as a staple in the luggage of almost every Sumi when they leave home for studies or work. If not packed and taken, it is definitely parcelled. Home is where ‘axone’ is.

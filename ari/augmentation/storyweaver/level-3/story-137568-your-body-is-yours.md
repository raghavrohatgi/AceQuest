# Your Body Is Yours

Did you know that no one in the world has a body exactly like yours? Our bodies share many similarities and differences, but each body is unique. Isn’t that incredible?

Your body may be big small round wobbly. square hairy

No matter what your body is like, it is very, very special. And your body belongs to you.

It's important to get to know your body well. After all, it's yours for life. Are you familiar with these parts of your body? Forehead Chin Shoulder Belly button Vulva Thigh Toe Ankle Chest Palm Nostril Elbow Buttock Penis

Your body changes as you grow older. Have you noticed this?

Your body may get bigger smaller rounder hairier squarer wobblier. No matter how your body changes, your&nbsp; body will always be very, very special.

Your body belongs to you, so tell someone if you’re uncomfortable when they touch you. Especially if it hurts, or doesn’t feel right to you.

It may not always seem that easy, but it’s perfectly fine to show your discomfort. You can say, “I don’t like it when you touch me.”&nbsp; Or “Don’t do that.” Or just a loud and firm “ NO! ” If they don’t listen to you, you can always walk away. Or even shout.

Remember, it doesn’t matter who it is. If you’re being touched in a way that makes you feel uncomfortable, you always have the right to say no. Even if it’s someone you love, or someone who says they love you. Because your body is yours alone, and no one else’s. Don’t let anyone make you feel otherwise.

If for some reason that person does not listen to you, don’t worry. It’s not your fault. It’s never your fault. Tell your parents what happened. Or tell any adult whom you trust and feel comfortable with. It could be a teacher, a grandparent, an aunt or an uncle. Someone who will listen patiently and find a way to protect you.

Because remember, your body belongs to you,&nbsp; and no one else. It doesn’t belong to your mother. Or your father. It doesn’t belong to your sister. Or your brother. Or your best friend. Or even your pet!

Your body belongs&nbsp; only to you.

Your body is precious, and so you must take good care of it. But it’s not always that simple, is it? Here are some questions and answers that may help you understand how best to protect your body.

Which are the parts of my body that no one should touch? No one should be allowed to touch or see your private parts. When you’re young, your parents may touch these parts when they give you a bath, or change you, or even when you’re unwell. As you grow older, you will be able to do all this on your own. A doctor or nurse may touch these parts when checking your body, but it’s best to have a parent around when this happens. This also means that it’s not right for you to see or touch anyone else’s private parts. Remember, private parts are private and private means that it is just for you.

Which are my private parts? Each of our bodies is unique. So think of it like this — &nbsp; anything that is covered by clothes in this picture is private.

I felt uncomfortable when someone touched me. But it wasn’t a private part. Is that okay? It’s NEVER OKAY for anyone to touch you in any way that makes you feel uncomfortable, even if it’s not a private part. At times like this, it’s best to say “I don’t like it when you do that.” Remember, your body belongs to you and no one else. If you ever feel confused about any kind of touch, talk to your parents, or to any adult whom you trust.

Is all touch unsafe? Definitely not! A kiss from a parent, a hug from a relative, holding your friend’s hand —&nbsp; these are all ways of showing that you care. But if you ever feel uncomfortable by any kind of touch, you can still say no. Which also means that if someone says no to you, you need to listen to them.

If someone has touched you in a way that hurt or made you feel uncomfortable, and you think you need help, you can also call 1098. 1098 will connect you to Childline, an organization that helps children in difficult situations.

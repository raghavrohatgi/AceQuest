# Muthu and Kappu

For as long as the Muthanna family of Kottageri, in Coorg, could remember, Gangu Muthanna and Raja Muthanna had wanted a pet.

But their father Muthanna did not. “It’s too wild. No!” “It’ll dirty the house. No!” “Who knows where it has been. NO!”

One day, Muthanna (whom we shall&nbsp; call Muthu) found that his children had brought home a kitten. A tiny BLACK kitten.

“Where did you find this thing?” Muthu asked. “Don’t you remember what happened to Machu Uncle? He was enjoying his evening walk when a black cat crossed his path. The next day he was dead!” “But Pappa, he was 93 years old.” “NO! Just take it back before something bad happens!” Aiyo. The children begged and pleaded, but he refused to budge.&nbsp; So they took the kitten back...

to their room, where they secretly started to take care of him. But soon enough, Muthu found out.

He was very, very angry. “NO!” he thundered. “We cannot keep it! Take it back to where it came from.”

Gangu and Raja were very, very sad. They refused to talk to their pappa or eat their favourite dinner of noolputtu and koli curry.

That night, Muthu tossed and turned. When he finally fell asleep, he dreamt of black, furry blobs. The next morning, he awoke to loud sounds coming from outside the house. GRRR GRRRRR Pappa!

“Muthu!” “Pappa, come fast!” Muthu ran out. “Pappa, it’s the kitten! He’s in the tree!&nbsp; We have to help him.” Muthu lo oked up.

Gangu, Raja and Muthu’s wife Poovi stared open-mouthed as Muthu took off his chappals. He then climbed carefully up the tree. The kitten looked into his eyes and mewed softly. M E W Muthu gently scooped the kitten into his sweater and climbed down.

“We need to take care of him, Pappa. He is so scared.” Muthu paused. Finally, he said, “OK. But once he gets better,&nbsp; you’ll have to find a home for him. A different home.”

Over the next week, Muthu watched the&nbsp; kitten from a distance. He was wild. He left fur everywhere. He even stole dosas right off their plates! But Muthu had never seen Gangu and Raja this happy. And sometimes, even he could admit that the kitten was rather funny. KAPPUUU KAPPU

Then, one day, as he was reading the morning paper, the kitten jumped up onto the chair and settled into Muthu’s lap, purring loudly.

Muthu opened his mouth to say NO. But he said nothing.

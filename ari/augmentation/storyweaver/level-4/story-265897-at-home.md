# At Home

Coor, coor, cooor... Ammini rubs the sleep out of her eyes as she wakes up to the call of the chembothu nesting in the mango tree.

“I didn’t keep my school uniform ready last night,” grumbles Ammini. “Oh, I forgot, I don’t have to go to school.” Achan is making coffee and idlis in the kitchen. Ammini runs downstairs and gives him a good-morning hug. She hopes that the family can go out for a walk today.

“The idlis are so good, Achan!” says Ammini. Her four-year-old brother Unni walks into the kitchen. “Kamala cheriyamma?” he calls out, sleepily. Ammini and Unni’s aunt is his favourite playmate.

“She is still at the hospital, Unni. She said that she would come home as soon as the sick people get better,” says Achan. Unni’s face crumples. Kamala cheriyamma is a nurse, and has not come home for weeks now.

“Come, Unni. Let’s see the flowers that have bloomed in the garden,” says Ammini.

“Look, there is the arali. See the yellow in the middle? The honeybees will come to carry that away. And look at the techi, see the big bunches? My teacher says it is called jungle flame in English.”

“Hello!” “Abu uncle, I like your mask,” Ammini says. Abu uncle salutes, and asks, “What are both of you doing?”&nbsp; “Looking at the flowers.&nbsp; Where is the police car?” Ammini asks.

“Still asleep after screaming with her siren all night long. Stay at home, otherwise she will wake up and say, weeeewoooweeeewooo,” says Abu uncle. Abu uncle waves and walks towards his mother Zeenat aunty’s house. It is almost time for Eid. Every year, Zeenat aunty makes delicious biryani and sheer korma. Will they be able to go for the feast this year, Ammini wonders.

“Ammini, time for school,” Amma says. “Good morning, Maya teacher!”&nbsp; Ammini’s classmates are waving at each other from the tiny squares on the laptop. School is so different now. Ammini’s best friend Nina is absent today. She shares the computer with her big brother, so she attends school twice a week.

Ammini misses Nina, but she misses school even more. She misses the bumpy rides to school in Gigi chettan’s rickshaw. She misses splashing in the puddles on the playground with Nina. She misses whispering and exchanging tiffin boxes at lunchtime. And her favourite set of colour pencils is still in school. When will she ever get it back?

School ends at lunchtime. “Chechi, can we go to the playground?” Unni asks Ammini. Ammini looks at Amma, who shakes her head. “Not today, Unni. But we can play the volcano lava game Amma showed us!” Ammini says.

Ammini and Unni race through the house gathering up cushions, pillows and blankets. They take their toy animals and arrange them around the room.&nbsp; “Come on, Unni, we have to rescue the animals. And remember, the floor is lava!”

“That was so much fun!” Ammini says as she and Unni lie on the blanket with their toys.&nbsp; “Yes, but I miss the slides and swings and grass, chechi.”

“Me too,” Ammini says. “I also miss the beach and the cotton candy and the balloons and going to Ammumma and Appoopan.” They imagine going for walks, running in the playground, hugging their grandparents tight.

The sun is an orange marble now.&nbsp; “Time for a walk, maybe,” Ammini thinks.&nbsp; It has been a while since she last put on her shoes. Amma and Achan are sitting on the sofa, listening to music. Ammini takes Unni’s hand and they run to squeeze in between Amma and Achan.&nbsp; “I hope we can go for a walk soon or to the beach. But until then, I’m glad that I can spend so much time with Unni, Achan and Amma,” Ammini thinks.

# Langpang Boy

In a high mountain village where trees touch the clouds, there lived a young boy. He was the village chief’s son. The boy was aggressive and reckless, afraid of no one. He would endlessly tease the younger children, pulling their ears and making them cry. In a high mountain village where trees touch the clouds, there lived a young boy. He was the village chief’s son. The boy was aggressive and reckless, afraid of no one. He would endlessly tease the younger children, pulling their ears and making them cry.

If chirping birds disturbed him while roaming the hillsides, he would destroy the birds’ nests.

Should a poor little squirrel catch his eye, he would chase it around and&nbsp; throw stones at it.&nbsp; And if he came across a poor little seedling, it would immediately find itself&nbsp; squashed to bits.

“Stop doing this! Show some kindness,” everyone asked.

“Try and stop me,” he replied arrogantly, knowing that they could not. The world was his to enjoy, and he cared for nothing and no one. He was a terror, not only for the village children but also for the birds and small animals that lived in the forest near his village.

One day, while napping in the shade&nbsp; of a big tree by the meadow, he was&nbsp; woken up by the village&nbsp; guardian spirit, the kameo . Annoyed&nbsp; at being disturbed, he rudely asked&nbsp; the kameo what it wanted.

Unaffected, the kameo said that it had a gift&nbsp; for him and presented two items in front of&nbsp; him.&nbsp; A beautiful shiny sword and a basket of rice.&nbsp; “You have a gift. Choose one of the items&nbsp; and the gift will be revealed to you,” the&nbsp; kameo calmly said.

The boy looked at the&nbsp; items, and his eyes&nbsp; immediately went to&nbsp; the shiny sword.&nbsp; After all, for someone&nbsp; like him, what could be&nbsp; better than a sword&nbsp; that represented&nbsp; courage and strength? However, right then his stomach growled, and his eyes went to the basket of rice. Deciding to try out both the items, the boy first picked up the sword.

“Very well then,” the kameo said, “now for your gift.”&nbsp; “Wait!” Before the boy could say anything further,&nbsp; the kameo took his hand and took a step.

Immediately the world around them&nbsp; changed. Instead of the wide blue skies, the air was filled with smoke and cries of suffering.&nbsp; The hills and fields lay barren, and the glint of swords clanging was the only thing&nbsp; that could be seen.

The faces of the people were grim or covered in tears, their skin sagging&nbsp; and bones visible. “Please give us something to&nbsp; eat!” “Go away! This is mine!” The ground was cracked, and all around, cries could be heard.

The boy was terrified. “What is this?” he cried. “Your future,” the kameo said. “By choosing the sword, by choosing what you think is courage and strength, you have chosen endless fighting and heartlessness. Now you get to live in this future.” “I don’t want this!” The boy cried, “I only touched the sword; I didn’t&nbsp; say I wanted it! I want the rice. I choose the basket of rice!” The kameo shook its head. “You have already made your choice. It&nbsp; cannot be changed.” The boy looked on in horror as the world burnt around him, helpless&nbsp; to do anything.

Koktui…koktui…koktui… The boy suddenly woke up and found himself back at the meadow. The hills were still green, the trees still provided shade, and the air was filled with the melodious sound of the birds. He was not angry with the birds but, for the first time he was grateful for their melodious sound.

He&nbsp; looked around and saw the exuberant bloom of flowers, whose beauty he had never seen before.

Amidst the pleasant shade and the cool breeze, the&nbsp; boy sat and cried.&nbsp; He cried in shame for his actions.&nbsp; He cried in guilt for the pain he had caused.&nbsp; He also cried in joy for the second chance he had&nbsp; been given.&nbsp; From then on, he was a changed boy.&nbsp; He couldn’t change his choice in the dream, but he&nbsp; could make different choices here.

He no longer pulled&nbsp; the ears of other&nbsp; children and never&nbsp; threw stones at the&nbsp; small animals. He&nbsp; apologised to&nbsp; everyone he had ever&nbsp; hurt. He enjoyed the&nbsp; melodious singing of&nbsp; the birds and actively&nbsp; began planting&nbsp; saplings.

He would even visit families in need and help&nbsp; them in chopping firewood, fetching water, and&nbsp; feeding the pigs.&nbsp; Everyone was surprised by his sudden change.

Then one day, a strong southwest wind blew out the roofs of&nbsp; several houses in the village, leaving chaos everywhere.&nbsp; To everyone’s surprise, the boy came up with the idea of&nbsp; organising&nbsp; a workforce&nbsp; called langpang by mobilising his friends&nbsp; and helped&nbsp; with the re-roofing&nbsp; works, turn by turn.&nbsp; “It’s not the roof in my house,” one of his friends&nbsp; complained,&nbsp; “why should I help?”&nbsp; “We should mind our own business,”&nbsp; an elder said.&nbsp; “This is our village and they are our&nbsp; neighbours,” the boy&nbsp; explained, “we must take care of each other.”

Even though some were initially hesitant, looking at the boy’s efforts, they soon realised the importance of the langpang and worked together to help each other and strengthen the community bonds. Since then, langpāng became a staple part of the village, whether it be during house construction, firewood cutting or agriculture. The village began to prosper, and the prosperity of the village spread in the region. And gradually, village after village began to adopt langpāng . As for the boy, he lovingly came to be known as the langpāng boy in his village. Everyone adored him. And every time the boy looked at his harmonious village,&nbsp; he only&nbsp; felt immensely grateful to the kameo ,&nbsp; who showed him&nbsp; the error of his ways.

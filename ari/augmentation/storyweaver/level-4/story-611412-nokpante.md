# Nokpante

Under the depths of land and sea, the mites * &nbsp; were whispering among themselves. “What is she making?” “Maybe it’s a new instrument.” “I think she’s probably building a–” * Mite: A supernatural being who is&nbsp; considered divine and sacred.

“It’s finally done!&nbsp; A house just for my sons and grandsons.&nbsp; I shall call this… Nokpante !”

“This calls for a celebration!” Sanima Minima look s up at the mites . &nbsp;“Come down, everyone! Let’s prepare for a housewarming feast. So many guests to invite!&nbsp; So many things to do!”

The mites came down one after another, all kinds of gifts in their arms. Some brought pigs and cows, while others carried chickens.

They sat around a huge table filled with an array of food and drinks. “Did we invite everyone?” Minima asks. “No, Minima. We are yet to invite humans.” “Oh! The last time a human came was when we were&nbsp; introducing our instruments, wasn’t it?” “Very well, then. I shall send a messenger.&nbsp; A worthy person shall join us soon.”

And so, a mermaid along with the Sangkini travels through dreams to find the perfect guest for the mites . &nbsp;&nbsp; After days of searching,&nbsp; they finally find a young man.

Deciding to test whether he was worthy, the fierce Sangkini pretends to attack the mermaid in front of Noro. “Stop. Stay back,” Noro bravely steps between them.&nbsp; They had found a worthy human. The mermaid tells Noro, “Young one, your bravery has reached the ears of the mites .&nbsp; They have invited you to join them.”&nbsp; Noro asks, “Join them where?” “You’ll find out soon. Come.”

Noro and the mermaid travel through the depths of land and sea and reach the home of the mites . Standing in front of Noro, Minima says, “Welcome, Noro. We’ve been waiting for you.”

Feeling like he was still dreaming, Noro stutters out, “What...what is this place?!” “This is Nokpante , Noro. A place where young men like yourself will live and learn.”

And so, for the next seven days, Noro lives and learns in the Nokpante .

He returns home and tells everyone about his time with the mites . From then, every village starts building their own Nokpante .

Activity There are 10 differences between the two Nokpante . Can you spot all of them?

About ' Nokpante ' &nbsp; According to the beliefs of the A’chik people (the people of the Garo community of Meghalaya), Nokpante was first built under the depths of land and sea. It was constructed by the goddess Sanima Minima, who had an uncountable number of children and grandchildren. The housewarming for the very first Nokpante was a grand celebration.&nbsp; A human named Noro Mande Dima Risi was invited. He witnessed the magnificence of the Nokpante , returned to his village, and taught his fellow villagers how to build it. From then on, Nokpante is known as the house of the bachelors, where bachelors live from as young as 8–9 years of age until they get married. It is built at the heart of A’chik villages near the Nokma’s &nbsp;(village chief) house. &nbsp; This is a fictionalised story based on the origins of the Nokpante .

'Nokpante,' is part of the 45 Meghalaya-contextual storybooks curated under The Forgotten Folklore Project (TFFP), an initiative of the Sauramandala Foundation.&nbsp; The TFFP team is grateful to Dr Colnat B Marak and Sir Sembertush A Sangma for sharing extensively about Nokpante, without which this book would not have been possible.

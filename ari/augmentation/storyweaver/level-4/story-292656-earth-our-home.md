# Earth, Our Home

The Earth's Story I am Earth, your home. A place to explore and roam, A place of wonder and love, With soil beneath and sky above. Choking on carbon, gasping for breath, Now is the time to protect Earth, your home, A place of wonder and love.

The Earth’s climate is changing! Our planet is now warmer, because humans are pumping more and more greenhouse gases into the atmosphere and, at the same time, cutting down more and more trees. Glaciers and ice caps are melting, the sea level is rising and we are seeing extreme weather events, such as floods and droughts, more frequently.

Hasdeo Arand: Hear My Story by Karthika Naïr Greetings, young ones who may not know my name. Age-old forest, I do not ask for fame! Lung of the earth I am, and of your land. My breath gives shape to river, ravine, strand and quicksand, tribe and tree, bird, beast, et al. Life I grant to aam, bamboo, mahua, sal… scores of herbs and scrubs and medicinal plants. Gods and Gonds, tigers, leopards, even ants, elephants, bears, and many more do roam on my chest. Since ancient times, I’m their home. Yes, home. Green and brown, golden, auburn: theirs through summer whirlwinds, earthquakes, monsoon scares, through joy, sorrow, the ravages of time. Together, yes, like a bell and its chime.

Hasdeo Arand: Now Do Something, Please Together we thought we would always be. But humans found black gold in my belly. A billion metric tonnes of coal, they say, run in my veins. For this fortune, they’ll slay — oh, gladly! — the fates of plants and seedpods, tigers and tribes, elephants, even gods. Know this: they tear into my flesh to reach the heart as I speak, deaf to my pained screech, blind to the hundreds of thousands of trees marked for death, one by one. Do something, please. Look, listen, query, while there is still light. Act, before it’s too late. Act, for my plight turns the sky to dust and rivers to ash. Act, and save yourselves from the Earth’s backlash.

Forests like Hasdeo Arand in Chhattisgarh, which is home to leopards, elephants and sloth bears, are being cleared for coal mining. Trees absorb carbon from the air, are reservoirs of water and help stabilize the climate. Cutting down forests adds more carbon to the atmosphere!

Rainforests in Indonesia and Malaysia are being cut down for oil palm cultivation. The orangutan and other animals’ homes are being threatened by habitat degradation and frightening wildfires. Changes in the climate increase the risk and severity of forest fire.

The Dolphin Lament There’s sewage in the water There’s garbage all around Boats and dams and blockages And fishing nets abound. You hunt me for my flesh and oil, You churn and chop the river With noises that can make me blind. Soon, I’ll be gone forever. My shushuk breath no longer heard — I will not dive nor leap. The water will lose all its wealth; The river, then, will weep.

The South Asian River Dolphin was once found in many river systems. This freshwater mammal is now endangered because of poaching, habitat loss and pollution. Clean rivers can reduce the impact of global warming.

The Pondicherry Shark Says Goodbye I’m small, I’m stocky, I’m dead. I rise to speak inside your head. Once, we were a regular catch But none of us was ever a match For your tricks, your greed, your skill At trapping and netting and making a kill. You turned us into shark-fin soup You threw us all into a mighty loop You ruined our homes and our ease By spilling oil on our rivers and seas. After forty years they found me — dead. I rise to speak inside your head. Don’t you see, our lives are linked? Save other sharks from going extinct. Do your bit for all — it’s now or never. From me, my friend, it’s goodbye, forever.

The Pondicherry Shark was considered extinct for almost 40 years, but was sighted once again in the waters of Sri Lanka. Critically endangered, the species needs protection from overfishing, habitat loss and pollution. Climate change is accelerating extinction. When a species goes extinct, entire ecosystems fall apart, affecting the balance of nature.

Our future, Our choice Climate change’s impact is felt by human beings, but also by animals, insects, birds, plants, and all forms of life. Scientists believe that the Earth has entered its sixth extinction period, one accelerated by climate change and human actions. But, we still have a choice. A choice for a better future. Small steps can go a long way in protecting life on planet Earth, including ours. Start with the most important one — respecting and loving nature. From this respect and love will emerge voices that speak for Nature, and from these voices will grow responsible actions and green policies that make meaningful change happen. Together we can face the climate crisis and build a better tomorrow. For you and everyone who calls planet Earth their home.

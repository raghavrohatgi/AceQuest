About the story Ultapani Forest Block Village is a biodiversity area situated under Holtugaon Forest Division of Manas Biosphere reserve situated in Kokrajhar district, Assam, India. The name literally translates as ‘The Reverse Water’ that flows through the forest from west to east, although other rivers most likely flow from east to west. It is close to the Indo-Bhutan border; settlements of Bodo and Nepali communities can be found in the area. Ultapani is known as the “Haven of Butterfly” and is the native habitat of several rare, threatened, and endangered (RET) species, including the Golden Langur and the well-known Great Pied Hornbill. Another asset of Ultapani Reserve Forest is its orchids. Unfortunately, the overuse of forest resources have put this forest's important asset under jeopardy. It negatively impacts the ecological balance and causes numerous issues for wild creatures. Ultapani is also a abode of spirits and deities, there are frequent occurrences of paranormal activities happening inside the forest and village areas. Jwngthi is based on a mythical dwarf spirit that resides in the jungle of Ultapani Village. Many people residing in the village are said to have encountered this spirit. The spirit is said to have not been harmful in anyway, but rather mischievous. It is believed that he has been living there for ages.

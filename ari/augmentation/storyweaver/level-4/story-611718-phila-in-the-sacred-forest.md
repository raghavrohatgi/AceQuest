# Phila in the Sacred Forest

The day Phila wore&nbsp; her shiny boots&nbsp; She made Chaos all around.

Stomp,&nbsp; Stomp,&nbsp; Stomp,&nbsp; Stomping her feet everywhere&nbsp; she goes.

" Tamsneng!” * her mother says. “No, I’ll never stop stomping!” Phila cries. *Tamsneng: willful

Full of anger, she stomps away. Stomp, Stomp, Stomp &nbsp; To the nearby Forest, she marches on.

Without a thought in mind, Phila tramps through the bush into the thick of the Forest. Stomp, Stomp, Sto…..

Until she trips and stumbles down a mossy mound!

Hurt and bruised, she kicks the root that tripped her. Instead, she misses and her boot goes flying into the air.

“Arrrggghhhhhhhhh!” cries a frustrated Phila, breaking the silence of the forest. Birds shriek,&nbsp; monkeys clatter,&nbsp; insects buzz, frogs yelp,&nbsp; and bats click.”

Having screamed, she suddenly realises something.&nbsp; She needs her boot! &nbsp;Without it how could she go&nbsp; Stomp, Stomp, Stomp? &nbsp; She runs in search of her boot!&nbsp; But it was nowhere in sight.

Phila slumps by a Stonehenge in despair, when she hears a&nbsp; soft deep whisper.&nbsp; "Is this what Chaos look like?”&nbsp; She opened her eyes to see a snake staring back at her with bright yellow eyes.

Phila jumps on the stones, terrified! “You can speak?” shocked, she asks. “Of course, I can!” says the Snake.&nbsp; “I’m not just any snake, I am the protector of this Forest”

“What do you protect?” asks Phila. “The sacred forestssss,” hisses the Snake,&nbsp; " f rom disruptors like Chaos. " Phila, embarrassed, tries to hide her foot. “Who are you calling Chaos? I just lost my boot” she says, pointing back.&nbsp;

" Is that so?” says the Snake. “Well, I happened to see a boot flying past my head when I was on top of that tree.&nbsp; It looked more like it was flung, than lost.” Upon hearing that, Phila’s breath hitched and she runs, leaving the snake behind.

She searched and searched for the boot until &nbsp;the sun had gone down.&nbsp; Tired and hungry, all Phila&nbsp; could think of was her mother’s hot bowl of soup. Unaware that a Leopard, the Forest’s deity had crept up behind her.&nbsp;

“How did you end up here?” asks the Leopard. "Wahhh!" Phila shrieks as she edges backwards, terrified.&nbsp; “You can speak too, like the Snake?”&nbsp;

" I can speak in many tongues, for you to understand,” the Leopard replies lazily. “Will...will you eat me up?” Phila asks, hesitating.&nbsp; The Leopard smiles and says, “Oh no no, I cannot harm. I only protect and provide guidance to those who need it the most.&nbsp; And you are looking for...? "

“My boot.” Phila replies before the Leopard could finish his question. “Oh!” says the Leopard.&nbsp; “So you're the one causing the angry stomps everywhere today." " I don't know what you're&nbsp; talking about, " Phila cries stubbornly.&nbsp; “Really?” the Leopard asks again,&nbsp; “They were some very loud stomps.&nbsp; The whole forest heard it.” Phila opens her mouth to respond, but she could say nothing more. She looks down, dejected.&nbsp; She really did go Stomp, Stomp, Stomp.

" Your boots, why are they important to you?” the Leopard asks. Still scared, Phila thinks about the question.&nbsp; “I like them! I can go anywhere with them.” “Even if stomping around is hurting everything around you?”

" What? I didn’t cause any harm…I…,” She couldn’t finish her words as she recalled her attempt to kick the roots. “Follow me,” says the Leopard as he turns to walk through a dark path.

" Today marks the bloom of this flower.&nbsp; It stabilizes the forest’s harmony. It blooms every fortnight as a way to keep the balance between people and nature,”&nbsp; the Leopard declares. Looking at the pitiful flowers, a sudden realization washes across Phila's face. " They are dying, aren't they? " she whispers. “

“Mmm…” the leopard says gently.&nbsp; “That's what Chaos does, causing a ripple effect throughout the Forest.&nbsp; The trees can tolerate it. standing firm. But these flowers bear the brunt of it all. " " If you carry anger in your heart, it disrupts the harmony around you.” Phila’s eyes fills with tears.

After a while Phila softly says,&nbsp; “I think I understand now. I don’t want to be Chaos anymore.” The Leopard smiles and shows his gnarly teeth. " Come now, I'll take you home, " he says. “Wait!”

Phila takes her remaining boot off and feels the warmth of the Earth beneath her bare feet. Placing the boot on the side, she looks up at the&nbsp; Leopard and smiles. “Now we can go.”

The Leopard leads Phila&nbsp; to her own doorstep. There, her mother was waiting,&nbsp; with a hot bowl of soup.

# Nungpak Thabi vs Tumurak

There is utter chaos at Pumlen lake today.

Tumurak and Nungpak Thabi are in a heated argument.

“A tiny rock like you can’t be considered a Hill! That’s all!” Tumurak stated.

“Shut up! Who are you to decide that?” Nunpak Thabi retorted.

“Hahahaha. What? Afraid of hearing the truth?” “What trut…”

“You may think you are big enough&nbsp; but that is just your ego! How’s the&nbsp; view from down there, little HILL?” “I may be—”

“Huh? Say what? Can’t hear you! Whisper it into my ears. Oh, wait. You can’t. BECAUSE YOU ARE A&nbsp; TINYYYYY ROCKKKKK!”

“Don’t mock me! I may be small, but I’m proud of who I am.” “You may have trees, Tumurak but I have history. Princess Thoibi herself once stood on me, casting her net into the water—”

“Ohhhhh so you are a fishing dock! And you are proud of it? How embarrassing!” “Hills like me are made to stand tall, to touch the skies, to feel the winds.” “Do you even understand what I am saying? Hmph?”

“Ahhhhhhh that’s enough!” “You think you’re better just because you’re tall? I’ll show you, Tumurak!&nbsp; I challenge you to a fight right here, right now!”

“A fight? Are you really going to challenge me, little rock? Because you’ll regret it, Nungpak Thabi.”

“Here! Take this! I bet you will not be able to withstand my power!”

“Is that all you got, Mighty Hill?” “Take this!” “Ah!&nbsp; What kind of trickery is this?”

“Ouch! Stop hitting! Alright! Alright! Stop!”

“Hmph, now how’s the view from down there?” “God, you are absolutely crazy!”

“Crazy? Oh I am not done. Hear me clearly, Tumurak!”

“From this day on,&nbsp; I will not be flooded by&nbsp; the lake unless you are flooded first.” “Ahhhhh!” “Ahhhhh!”

“No matter how fierce the storms.” “No matter how high the waters rise…” Ahhhhh!

“I will never be submerged until you, the great Tumurak, are completely immersed by the lake.&nbsp;That is my promise!” “Stop! Stop! I give up!”

“I am sorry, Nungpak Thabi. Can you please forgive me?” “Only if you promise to never mock me or anyone else again.”

“I promise. I have learnt my lesson. I am sorry for mocking you, Nungpak Thabi.” “That is all I wanted. C’mon now, get up. Let’s clean up our mess here.”

From then on, peace prevailed in Pumlen lake as the two hills stood together, overlooking it. While the two never fought again, Nungpak Thabi’s promise stands true, even today.

No matter how fierce the storm is or how high the waters rise, unless Tumurak gets flooded, not a single drop touches Nungpak Thabi.

Glossary •&nbsp; Princess Thoibi: The story of Princess Thoibi of Moirang is one of the most celebrated and romantic epics of Manipur, intertwining themes of love, sacrifice, and defiance against societal norms. It tells the passionate and tragic love story between Thoibi, the beautiful and strong-willed princess of Moirang, and Khamba, a brave but orphaned young man from the Khuman clan. • Nungpak Thabi: Nungpak Thabi is a massive boulder located at the base of Tumurak, near the shores of Pumlen Lake. The locals consider it a hill, and its name is derived from the materials and shape of the rock: ‘nungpak’ meaning ‘flat rock’ and ‘Thabi’ meaning ‘to lay down’ in&nbsp; Meiteilon.&nbsp; Composed of slate, the boulder is believed to be ancient and is steeped in&nbsp; history and&nbsp; legend.&nbsp; One such tale connects it to Princess Thoibi, a legendary figure&nbsp; from Moirang,&nbsp; who is&nbsp; said to have visited the rock in ancient times. • Tumurak: Tumurak is a hill located in Kakching Khunou, rising prominently at the edge of Pumlen Lake. The hill is densely covered with native vegetation and serves as a habitat for various wildlife. It is also a popular spot for local people who visit to forage and gather firewood.

About the story The story of Nungpak Thabi and Tumurak is a popular&nbsp; folklore Kakching Khunou, which tells of a rivalry between&nbsp; the towering hill Tumurak and the small boulder Nungpak Thabi,&nbsp; located near Pumlen Lake. Once widely known, this tale symbolised&nbsp; defiance and the power of belief, with locals believing that Nungpak Thabi&nbsp; never got flooded despite being near the lake. However, the geographical structure of Nungpak Thabi has become almost unrecognizable today due to encroachment and neglect. There is an urgent need for conservation efforts to protect this cultural and natural site, preserving its history and ecological significance.

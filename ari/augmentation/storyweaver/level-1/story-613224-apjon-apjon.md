# Apjon Apjon

The cats and the dogs are sowing maize.

Even if they sow, even if they sow,

They can never eat it, though.

Tickles, tickles, tickles...

About 'ApJon' 'Apjon,' is a rhyme popularly sung in the Jaiñtia Hills whenever children see shapes in the clouds. They gather happily to sing it when the fluffy clouds fill the sky. This fun song celebrates the different shapes they see, making them feel excited and curious about the sky above.&nbsp; This timeless song is a joyful part of the culture and reminds people to appreciate the wonders of nature.

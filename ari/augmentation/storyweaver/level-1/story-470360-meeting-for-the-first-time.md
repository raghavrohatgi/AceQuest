# Meeting for the First Time

“ Aama, * what is that place?” I ask. “Our new home, Yangjee,” Aama replies. *Aama means mother in many languages spoken in Nepal

”Wow, these windows are big,” I say. “ Dai, * where is my doll?” “I do not know, Yangjee,” Dai replies. *Dai means big brother in many languages spoken in Nepal

I look for my doll. “My pretty little doll, where are you?” I call.

I see a small girl. The girl looks at me from behind the landlady. I want to talk to her.

“What is your favorite food?” I ask. “What is your name?” I am surprised. The girl is quiet.

“She is shy,” the landlady tells me. ”Her name is Lucky.” “Oh! Lucky!” I say. “What a nice name!”

I take Lucky’s hand. We run outside. ”Lucky, what do you like to play?” I ask. Lucky stays silent.

”Why does she not say anything?” I wonder. I am annoyed. But I want to be Lucky’s friend.

“Here is your doll, Nani, * ” Dai says through the window. ”Yay!” I dance happily with the doll. *Nani means little girl in Nepali

Lucky finally speaks. “I like to play with dolls,” she says. Lucky and I dance happily.

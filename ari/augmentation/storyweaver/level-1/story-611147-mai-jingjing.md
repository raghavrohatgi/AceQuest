# Mai Jingjing

Mai jingjing,&nbsp; Mai jingjing Ambi mikki

Mai jingjing Mai jingjing Atchu mikki

If you open your mouth and laugh haha A fly will enter&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp;

The one who sits quiet Is a lazy miser

For the lazy one There's chicken feet

The hardworking one&nbsp; Is b lessed with chicken drumstick

Singing meng meng Two cicadas

Along with the wind&nbsp; Hanging f rom the bamboo grove

Placed on top of the hand Jing jing finger

Everyone’s laughing haha Jing jing finger

Mai jingjing,&nbsp; Mai jingjing Ambi mikki Mai jingjing,&nbsp; Mai jingjing Atchu mikki

About 'Mai Jingjing' Mai Jingjing is a fun game played by children in the Garo Hills. They pinch their left hand with their right hand and say “mai jingjing, mai jingjing,” mimicking the flight of cicadas. They sing “ambi mikki” and “atchu mikki” and playfully tease each other. This rhyme, which has been reimagined and extended upon by the author, paints a picture of joy, keeping alive the memories of childhood for those who grew up in the Garo Hills.

Glossary Mai jingjing: Cicadas Ambi Mikki: Garo for 'Grandma with sleepies.' Atchu mikki: Garo for 'Grandpa with sleepies.'

# Let's Dance with Ainam

Hello, I am Ainam. I am practising the dance steps for the Shad Suk Mynsiem festival.

Mei taught me these dance steps.

Mei says that this is one of the oldest dances of the Khasi people. &nbsp; Ka Pahsyntiew, the legendary Chieftess of the Khasi people, taught us the dance.

First, you must stand straight, &nbsp; raise &nbsp; your right foot like this then move it forward.

Then move your left foot slowly. &nbsp; Move only your feet forward. &nbsp; Do not move your body.

Do it again! &nbsp; Right foot, left foot - like a crawl.

Be slow and steady. &nbsp; It is tricky but be patient.

Very nice! &nbsp; You and I are doing so well.

The Ka Shad Suk Mynsiem is a dance to thank God for blessing us. It's a way for us to say "thanks" through movement.

Now! Let's get ready to dance at the festival. &nbsp; Come join me.

See you at the Shad Suk Mynsiem!

Glossary Ainam: &nbsp; &nbsp; Implies “to give praise” or “to be known” in Khasi. It is also the name of the protagonist of the story. Mei: &nbsp; A Khasi word for mother. Shad Suk Mynsiem: &nbsp; Implies “dance with a joyous soul,” and is one of the most well-known cultural celebrations of the Khasi community in Meghalaya. Ka Pahsyntiew: Believed to be one of the most notable figures in Khasi folklore and the legendary queen of the Shillong chiefdom.

About Shad Suk Mynsiem Shad Suk Mynsiem or “dance with a joyous soul” is a well-known cultural celebration of the Khasi community in Meghalaya, India. It is a festival, where young male and female members of the community are dressed in bright colours with gold and silver ornaments to perform the annual dance. They come together to celebrate the blessings of God and honour Him through dance, music and prayers.

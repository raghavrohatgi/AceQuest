# Missing a Friend

Yangjee is riding a bus to the&nbsp; village to visit her aunt.&nbsp; She is sad because she didn’t get to tell her best friend, Lucky, that she was leaving for a few days.

“ Lhasso, * &nbsp; Aaru , * ” Yangjee says hello to her aunt.&nbsp; “Come inside and have some food, Aanga . * ” *lhasso-hello *Aaru- aunt (mother's younger sister) *Aanga-&nbsp; a term of endearment used for little girls

“Wow, look at all the roti , * &nbsp; mohi , * and sukuti . * &nbsp;If Lucky were here, her mouth would water like mine.” *roti- a round, crispy, puffy bread common in Nepal *mohi- &nbsp;yogurt drink or buttermilk drink *sukuti- meat jerky

“Come, Aanga ,” Yangjee’s aunt says. ”Let’s go for a walk around the village.”

“Oh, look at that chungi&nbsp; * made of leaves,” Yangjee says. *Chungi- a game similar to Hacky Sack played with a ball of rubber bands. It is played in Nepal and Northeastern India.

“If Lucky were here, we would have so much fun playing hide-and-seek.”

“Look! There are so many fish in the river,” Yangjee says.

“If Lucky were here, we would splash around in the water.”

Yangjee misses Lucky. “I wonder if Lucky is thinking about me, too.”

In the morning, it is time for Yangjee to leave. ”Goodbye, Aaru ,” she says to her aunt.

﻿ When she gets home, she says, “ Aama * &nbsp;I’m going to see Lucky.” *Aama means mother

“I’m sorry I didn’t tell you I was leaving, Lucky,” Yangjee tells her friend.

Lucky is quiet.&nbsp; “Please say something,” Yangjee says.

“I missed you so much, Yangjee, and I thought about you every day,” Lucky says. Yangjee hugs her best friend. ”Here, I brought your favorite cream biscuits,” she says.

”See what else I brought for you!” Yangjee says. “Wow, mangoes and bananas from the village!” Lucky exclaims.

Helen Keller was born on June 27, 1880, in Tuscumbia, Alabama. At 19 months old, she contracted an illness that left her both deaf and blind. Her early childhood was a time of frustration and confusion, unable to communicate with the world around her.
Everything changed when Anne Sullivan arrived as her teacher in 1887. Through remarkable patience and innovative teaching methods, Sullivan helped Keller understand that objects had names. The moment Keller understood that the word 'water' meant the cool liquid flowing over her hand became one of the most famous moments in educational history.

# Grid Game — Treasure Hunt

In this game, one player has to think of an object from the grid and help the other player locate it.

Let's see how Jagat and Mini are playing.

![img-29.jpeg](img-29.jpeg)

![img-30.jpeg](img-30.jpeg)

Mini takes 2 steps to the left and 1 step upwards. She reaches the Mark Mini's position on the grid.

![img-31.jpeg](img-31.jpeg)

![img-32.jpeg](img-32.jpeg)

What object did Jagat think of?

Note for Teachers: How to play—There are objects placed in a $4 \times 4$ grid. The first player helps the second player to reach the object he/she has thought of by giving clues. The players can move up, down, left or right. No diagonal movements are allowed. Children can play this game in pairs. Let student write/speak about the paths: provide them with prompts/questions/fillers, such as from the starting point, I moved _steps left/right. Then I moved _steps up/down and so on.

Play the game with your partner.

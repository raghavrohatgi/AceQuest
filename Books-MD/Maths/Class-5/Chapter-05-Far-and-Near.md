# Multiplying and Dividing Lengths

1. We need a $1\mathrm{m}80\mathrm{cm}$ cloth to make a shirt for a 10-year old child. How much cloth will be needed to make shirts for 20 such children?

$20 \times 1 \mathrm{~m} 80 \mathrm{~cm} = 20 \times 1 \mathrm{~m}$ and $20 \times 80 \mathrm{~cm}$

$$
\begin{array}{l}
= 20 \mathrm{~m} + 1600 \mathrm{~cm} \\
= 20 \mathrm{~m} + 16 \mathrm{~m} \\
= 36 \mathrm{~m}.
\end{array}
$$

Break the quantity into m and cm and multiply. You can also convert it into cm and multiply.

2. A shop sells cloth for making bags at ₹100 for 5 m. How much money is needed to buy a 1 m cloth?

If 5 m cloth costs ₹100, then a 1 m cloth costs 100 ÷ 5 = ₹20.

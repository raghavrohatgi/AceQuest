# More Operations on Weight

1. A farmer weighs a sack of potatoes and finds it to be $10\,\mathrm{kg}\,500\,\mathrm{g}$. If the farmer has 4 such potato sacks, what is the total weight of all the sacks?

$$
\begin{array}{l}
4 \times 10\,\mathrm{kg}\,500\,\mathrm{g} \\
= 4 \times 10\,\mathrm{kg} \text{ and } 4 \times 500\,\mathrm{g} \\
= 40\,\mathrm{kg} + 2000\,\mathrm{g} \\
= 40\,\mathrm{kg} + 2\,\mathrm{kg} = 42\,\mathrm{kg}.
\end{array}
$$

You can find the product by multiplying the kg and g separately and adding the two. You can also convert the quantity into grams and then multiply.

2. A box of nuts weighing $4\,\mathrm{kg}\,800\,\mathrm{g}$ is equally distributed into 4 smaller boxes. What is the weight of each small box in grams?

$$
\begin{array}{l}
4\,\mathrm{kg} \div 4 = 1\,\mathrm{kg} \\
800\,\mathrm{g} \div 4 = 200\,\mathrm{g} \\
\text{So, } 4\,\mathrm{kg}\,800\,\mathrm{g} \div 4 = 1\,\mathrm{kg}\,200\,\mathrm{g}
\end{array}
$$

We can also convert the quantity into grams and divide

$$
4800 \div 4 = ?
$$

# Chapter 3

## The Crow

There once was a crow.
He was black, as you know.
How to be beautiful,
he wanted to know.
Colourful feathers,
he wanted to grow.

He found one peacock
feather.
He stuck it in his tail.
Then he found another,
He picked up all without fail.

He stuck them in his wings,
Put one on his head.
I am now beautiful,
He danced and said.

The other crows laughed at him.
“Oh, what a fool you are!
You are beautiful in black.
We love you for what you are.”

Note to the teacher

- Use the poem to sensitise children that there are many skin tones in India. It is not the fair or dark complexion that matters, real beauty lies in good qualities.
- Help children talk about the qualities of a crow. Also discuss the uniqueness of different animals and birds.
- Ask children to speak about one good quality of their friend.
